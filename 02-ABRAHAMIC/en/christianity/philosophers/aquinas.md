# Thomas Aquinas (Sanctus Thomas Aquinas) | 토마스 아퀴나스

> The Angelic Doctor: Faith and Reason, Natural Law, and the Five Ways

---

## Overview | 개요

Saint Thomas Aquinas (1225–1274) was an Italian Dominican friar, philosopher, Catholic priest, and Doctor of the Church. He was an immensely influential philosopher, theologian, and jurist in the tradition of scholasticism. His monumental work, the *Summa Theologica*, remains the most comprehensive synthesis of Christian theology and Aristotelian philosophy.

> Aquinas is best known for his five arguments for the existence of God (the "Five Ways"), his doctrine of natural law, and his synthesis of faith and reason. He demonstrated that faith and reason are not opposed but complementary paths to truth.

He was declared a Doctor of the Church by Pope Pius V in 1567 and is considered the Catholic Church's greatest theologian and philosopher.

> 아퀴나스는 신 존재에 대한 다섯 가지 논증 ("다섯 가지 길"), 자연법 교리, 신앙과 이성의 종합으로 가장 잘 알려져 있습니다. 그는 신앙과 이성이 대립하지 않고 진리로의 상호보완적인 길임을 증명했습니다.

## The Five Ways | 다섯 가지 길
> In the *Summa Theologica*, Aquinas presents five arguments for God's existence, known as the "Five Ways" (*Quinque Viae*).

| Way | Argument | Conclusion |
|-----|----------|------------|
| **First Way** | Motion | There must be an Unmoved Mover |
| **Second Way** | Causation | There must be a First Cause |
| **Third Way** | Contingency | There must be a Necessary Being |
| **Fourth Way** | Gradation | There must be a Maximum Being |
| **Fifth Way** | Teleology | There must be an Intelligent Designer |

> "The existence of God can be proved in five ways... The first and more manifest way is the argument from motion."

> 『신학대전』에서 아퀴나스는 "다섯 가지 길" (*Quinque Viae*)로 알려진 신 존재에 대한 다섯 가지 논증을 제시합니다.
| 길 | 논증 | 결론 |
|----|------|------|
| **첫째 길** | 운동 | 부동의 원동자가 있어야 함 |
| **둘째 길** | 인과 | 제1원인이 있어야 함 |
| **셋째 길** | 우연성 | 필연적 존재가 있어야 함 |
| **넷째 길** | 등급 | 최대 존재가 있어야 함 |
| **다섯째 길** | 목적론 | 지적 설계자가 있어야 함 |

## Natural Law | 자연법
> Aquinas developed the most influential theory of natural law in the Western tradition. Natural law is the participation of the eternal law in rational creatures.

> "The natural law is nothing else than the rational creature's participation in the eternal law."

Four types of law:
1. **Eternal Law**: God's rational plan for creation
2. **Natural Law**: Human participation in eternal law
3. **Human Law**: Laws made by human authority
4. **Divine Law**: Revealed law in Scripture

> Natural law is knowable by reason and universal to all humans. Its first precept: "Good is to be done and pursued, and evil is to be avoided."

> 아퀴나스는 서양 전통에서 가장 영향력 있는 자연법 이론을 발전시켰습니다. 자연법은 이성적 피조물 안에서 영원법의 참여입니다.
> "자연법은 이성적 피조물의 영원법 참여에 다름 아니다."

## Faith and Reason | 신앙과 이성
> Aquinas argued that faith and reason are not opposed but complementary. Some truths are accessible to reason alone (e.g., God's existence), while others require revelation (e.g., the Trinity).

> "Grace does not destroy nature but perfects it."

> "Philosophy is the handmaiden of theology." Reason can lead to the threshold of faith, though faith transcends reason.

The harmony:
- Reason can demonstrate some divine truths
- Revelation confirms and extends rational knowledge
- Faith and reason cannot contradict each other
- Both come from the same source: God

> 아퀴나스는 신앙과 이성이 대립하지 않고 상호보완적이라고 주장했습니다. 일부 진리는 이성만으로 접근할 수 있고 (예: 신의 존재), 다른 것들은 계시를 필요로 합니다 (예: 삼위일체).
> "은총은 자연을 파괴하지 않고 완성한다."

## The Summa Theologica | 신학대전
> The *Summa Theologica* is Aquinas's masterwork, intended as a manual for beginners in theology. It addresses virtually every major question in Christian theology and philosophy.

Structure:
- **Part I**: God, creation, angels, humans
- **Part II-I**: Happiness, human acts, virtues, law
- **Part II-II**: Specific virtues and vices
- **Part III**: Christ, sacraments (incomplete)

> Aquinas died before completing the *Summa*. Near the end of his life, he had a mystical experience after which he said, "All that I have written seems like straw compared to what has now been revealed to me."

> 『신학대전』은 아퀴나스의 대작으로, 신학 초심자를 위한 지침서로 의도되었습니다. 그것은 기독교 신학과 철학의 거의 모든 주요 질문을 다룹니다.
