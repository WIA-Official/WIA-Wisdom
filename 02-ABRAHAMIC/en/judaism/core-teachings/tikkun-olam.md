# Tikkun Olam | 티쿤 올람 (תיקון עולם)

> Repairing the World

---

## Overview | 개요

"Tikkun olam" (Hebrew for "world repair") has come to connote social action and the pursuit of social justice. The term is a concept in Judaism that is generally translated as "healing the world" and has had multiple interpretations over the past 2,000 years.

In modern ideology and conversations within Judaism, tikkun olam indicates attempts to alleviate human suffering and avoid global catastrophes, encompassing multiple causes including social justice, human rights, environmentalism, and combating antisemitism.

---
## Historical Development | 역사적 발전

### Rabbinic Origins:
> "The phrase does not appear in the Hebrew Bible but first appears in rabbinic texts, such as the Talmud and Mishna, of the early centuries CE."

> "Its earliest appearance in the tradition sits in the Mishnah where the Sages use the phrase 'mipnei tikkun ha-olam' to justify legal rulings that preserve social stability—clarifying divorce law, regulating debt, preventing injustices."

### The Aleinu Prayer:
> "It was first invoked in a theological context in the ʿAleinu prayer, written in the 3rd century CE."

### Kabbalistic Development:
> "Isaac Luria, a leading kabbalistic thinker and rabbi, was disturbed by the problem of evil in the world. To explain, Luria expounded a mythic story of 'the broken vessels'—essentially, evil comes into the world because divine sparks that were part of God's creative light became trapped in shards of broken vessels."

### 랍비적 기원:
> "이 구절은 히브리 성경에 나타나지 않지만 기원 초기 세기의 탈무드와 미쉬나와 같은 랍비 문헌에 처음 나타납니다."
### 알레이누 기도:
> "3세기 CE에 쓰인 알레이누 기도에서 처음으로 신학적 맥락에서 사용되었습니다."
### 카발라적 발전:
> "이삭 루리아는 세상의 악의 문제에 고민했습니다. 설명하기 위해 루리아는 '깨진 그릇'의 신화적 이야기를 설명했습니다."

## Modern Transformation | 현대적 변형

> "The phrase 'tikkun olam' was first used to refer to social action work in the 1950s."

### Key Figures:
> "Perhaps the first Jewish thinker to use the phrase 'tikkun olam' in the modern sense of 'fixing the world' by building a just society was Rabbi Abraham Isaac Kook (1865–1935)."

> "Rabbi Gerald Serotta, who founded the progressive organization New Jewish Agenda (1980–92) was instrumental in the term's popularization. This new conceptualization of tikkun olam spread throughout most branches of Judaism."

### Current Usage:
> "In subsequent decades, many organizations and thinkers have used the term to refer to social action programs, tzedakah (charitable giving), gemilut hasadim (acts of kindness), and progressive Jewish approaches to social issues."

> "'티쿤 올람'이라는 구절은 1950년대에 사회 활동 작업을 지칭하는 데 처음 사용되었습니다."
### 현재 사용:
> "이후 수십 년 동안 많은 조직과 사상가들이 이 용어를 사회 활동 프로그램, 체다카(자선 기부), 게밀루트 하사딤(친절한 행위), 사회 문제에 대한 진보적 유대적 접근을 지칭하는 데 사용했습니다."

## Three Pillars | 세 기둥

> "Maimonides writes that tikkun olam requires efforts in all three of the great 'pillars' of Judaism: Torah study, acts of kindness, and the ritual commandments."

| Pillar | Hebrew | Focus |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Torah Study** | Talmud Torah | Learning and wisdom |
| **Acts of Kindness** | Gemilut Hasadim | Compassionate action |
| **Ritual Commandments** | Mitzvot | Sacred observance |

> "In Jewish thought, both ethical and ritual mitzvot are important to the process of tikkun olam."

> "마이모니데스는 티쿤 올람이 유대교의 세 가지 위대한 '기둥' 모두에서의 노력을 필요로 한다고 씁니다: 토라 학습, 친절한 행위, 의례적 계명."

## Covenantal Partnership | 언약적 동반자 관계

> "Jewish religion is a covenantal partnership. God is a partner, but humans must do their share in repairing the world to bring about redemption."

> "Judaism combines a liberal, utopian, universal vision of completely transforming the planet with a conservative, realistic, particularist method of transformation."

### The Human Role:
- Partner with God in creation
- Active agents of change
- Responsible for justice
- Obligated to heal brokenness

> "유대 종교는 언약적 동반자 관계입니다. 하나님은 동반자이지만, 인간은 구속을 가져오기 위해 세상을 고치는 데 자신의 몫을 해야 합니다."

## Contemporary Debates | 현대 논쟁

> "The modern popularity and ubiquity of tikkun olam has sparked some backlash within Jewish circles. Critics express regret about the term's overuse, concern that it has supplanted other key religious beliefs and obligations in Judaism, and fear that it has reduced Judaism to mere social activism and liberalism."

> "Those who remain committed to tikkun olam as an ethical ideal respond that healing the world and maintaining Judaism are not mutually exclusive but rather complimentary endeavors."

> "티쿤 올람의 현대적 인기와 편재성은 유대인 집단 내에서 일부 반발을 일으켰습니다. 비판자들은 용어의 과용에 대한 후회, 그것이 유대교의 다른 핵심 종교적 믿음과 의무를 대체했다는 우려, 유대교를 단순한 사회 활동과 자유주의로 축소했다는 두려움을 표현합니다."
