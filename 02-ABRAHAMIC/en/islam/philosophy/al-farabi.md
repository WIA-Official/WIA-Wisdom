# Al-Farabi (الفارابي) | 알파라비

> The Second Teacher: The Virtuous City, Prophecy, and the Harmony of Plato and Aristotle

---

## Overview | 개요

Abu Nasr al-Farabi (c. 872–950 CE), known as "the Second Teacher" (after Aristotle), was a renowned early Islamic philosopher who made major contributions to political philosophy, metaphysics, logic, and philosophy of music. He is particularly known for his attempt to harmonize the philosophies of Plato and Aristotle.

> Al-Farabi's *The Virtuous City* (*al-Madina al-Fadila*) adapts Plato's *Republic* to an Islamic context, describing the ideal state ruled by a philosopher-prophet who combines philosophical wisdom with prophetic revelation.

His work profoundly influenced later Islamic philosophers, including Avicenna and Averroes, as well as Jewish and Christian scholastics.

> 알파라비의 『덕 있는 도시』 (*알-마디나 알-파딜라*)는 플라톤의 『국가』를 이슬람적 맥락에 적응시켜, 철학적 지혜와 예언적 계시를 결합한 철학자-예언자가 통치하는 이상적인 국가를 묘사합니다.
---
## The Virtuous City | 덕 있는 도시
> Al-Farabi's masterwork describes the ideal city-state governed by philosophical principles. The virtuous city is an organic whole, like a healthy body, where each part performs its proper function.

> "The virtuous city resembles a perfect and healthy body, all of whose members cooperate to make the life of the animal perfect."

Structure of the city:
- **Ruler**: The philosopher-prophet who knows truth
- **Assistants**: Those who implement the ruler's vision
- **Guardians**: Those who protect the city
- **Masses**: Those who perform necessary functions

> 알파라비의 대작은 철학적 원리에 의해 통치되는 이상적인 도시 국가를 묘사합니다. 덕 있는 도시는 건강한 몸처럼 유기적인 전체로, 모든 부분이 적절한 기능을 수행합니다.
---
## Philosophy and Prophecy | 철학과 예언
> Al-Farabi's most innovative contribution was his theory of prophecy as a natural perfection of the human soul.

> The prophet receives truth through imagination and revelation, while the philosopher attains it through rational demonstration. Both reach the same truth, but by different paths.

The prophet-philosopher:
- Receives truth from the Active Intellect
- Communicates through images and symbols
- Governs the ideal city
- Unites contemplation and action

> "The prophet is a philosopher who speaks in images; the philosopher is a prophet who speaks in demonstrations."

> 알파라비의 가장 혁신적인 기여는 인간 영혼의 자연적 완성으로서의 예언 이론이었습니다.
> 예언자는 상상력과 계시를 통해 진리를 받고, 철학자는 이성적 논증을 통해 그것에 도달합니다. 둘 다 같은 진리에 도달하지만 다른 길로.
---
## Harmonizing Plato and Aristotle | 플라톤과 아리스토텔레스의 조화
> Al-Farabi wrote a treatise on the "Harmony of Plato and Aristotle," arguing that apparent contradictions between them are superficial.

> "The purpose of both is the same, and there is no disagreement between them."

His synthesis:
- Plato and Aristotle seek the same truth
- Differences are in method, not substance
- Both emphasize reason and virtue
- Neoplatonic emanation bridges them

> 알파라비는 "플라톤과 아리스토텔레스의 조화"에 대한 논문을 써서 그들 사이의 명백한 모순이 피상적이라고 주장했습니다.
> "둘의 목적은 같으며, 그들 사이에는 불일치가 없다."
---
## Emanation and the Intellects | 유출과 지성들
> Al-Farabi adopted a Neoplatonic cosmology of emanation, where being flows from the First Cause through a hierarchy of intellects.

The emanation:
1. **The One/First Cause** →
2. **First Intellect** →
3. **Celestial Spheres** →
4. **Active Intellect** →
5. **Human Soul**

> The Active Intellect illuminates human minds, enabling knowledge. This cosmology influenced Avicenna and later Islamic philosophy.

> 알파라비는 존재가 제1원인에서 지성들의 위계를 통해 흘러나오는 신플라톤적 유출 우주론을 채택했습니다.
---
## Music and Logic | 음악과 논리학
> Al-Farabi was also a master musician and wrote the influential *Grand Book of Music* (*Kitab al-Musiqi al-Kabir*).

> He saw music as both a mathematical and psychological science, connecting harmony with the order of the cosmos and the soul.

He also wrote extensively on logic, classifying the sciences and explaining how logic underlies all rational inquiry.

> 알파라비는 또한 음악의 대가였고 영향력 있는 『음악 대전』 (*키탑 알-무시키 알-카비르*)을 썼습니다.
