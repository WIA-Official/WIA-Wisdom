# Universal Principles | 보편 원칙

> 弘益人間: The Common Ground of 5,000 Years of Human Wisdom
> 홍익인간: 5천년 인류 지혜의 공통 기반

---

## Introduction | 서론

**English:**
Across five millennia, from the Vedas of ancient India (c. 1500 BCE) to the Analects of Confucius (c. 500 BCE), from the dialogues of Plato (c. 380 BCE) to the insights of modern science, humanity has sought to understand the nature of reality, the meaning of existence, and the path to a good life. Despite vast differences in culture, language, and historical context, certain principles emerge repeatedly across traditions—not because they were borrowed, but because they reflect fundamental truths about human experience.

> "Truth is one; the wise call it by many names." (*Ekam sat viprā bahudhā vadanti*) — *Rig Veda* I.164.46 (c. 1500 BCE)

These universal principles are not abstractions but living insights that have guided billions of human beings toward wisdom, compassion, and liberation.

**한국어:**
5천년에 걸쳐, 고대 인도의 베다 (기원전 약 1500년)에서 공자의 논어 (기원전 약 500년)까지, 플라톤의 대화 (기원전 약 380년)에서 현대 과학의 통찰까지, 인류는 현실의 본성, 존재의 의미, 좋은 삶으로의 길을 이해하고자 했습니다. 문화, 언어, 역사적 맥락의 광대한 차이에도 불구하고, 특정 원칙들이 전통을 넘어 반복적으로 출현합니다—그것들이 차용되었기 때문이 아니라 인간 경험에 대한 근본적 진리를 반영하기 때문입니다.

> "진리는 하나이다; 현자들은 그것을 여러 이름으로 부른다." — 『리그 베다』 I.164.46

## I. The Principle of Unity | 일체의 원리

### 萬物一體 — All Things Are One

**English:**
The most fundamental insight across wisdom traditions is that beneath the apparent diversity of existence lies a profound unity. This is not merely a philosophical claim but an experiential realization attained through contemplation, meditation, and mystical experience.

> "Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is One." — *Shema* (Deuteronomy 6:4)

> "All this is Brahman." (*Sarvam khalvidam brahma*) — *Chāndogya Upaniṣad* 3.14.1

> "The Tao that can be spoken is not the eternal Tao... These two spring from the same source but differ in name; this appears as darkness. Darkness within darkness. The gate to all mystery." — *Tao Te Ching* 1

> "There is no god but God." (*Lā ilāha illā Allāh*) — Islamic *Shahada*

> "Form is emptiness; emptiness is form." — *Heart Sutra*

**Manifestations Across Traditions:**

| Tradition | Expression of Unity |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Hinduism** | Brahman—the one reality behind all appearances |
| **Buddhism** | Śūnyatā—emptiness as the interconnected nature of all |
| **Judaism** | Ein Sof—the infinite beyond all attributes |
| **Christianity** | The Trinity—three persons, one God |
| **Islam** | Tawhid—absolute divine unity |
| **Taoism** | Tao—the nameless source of all |
| **Neoplatonism** | The One—beyond being itself |
| **Modern Physics** | Unified field theories; quantum entanglement |

**Practical Implication:**
If all is one, then harming another is harming oneself. The Golden Rule in all its forms flows naturally from this recognition.

**한국어:**
지혜 전통에 걸친 가장 근본적인 통찰은 존재의 겉보기 다양성 아래에 심오한 통일성이 있다는 것입니다. 이것은 단순히 철학적 주장이 아니라 관조, 명상, 신비 체험을 통해 도달한 체험적 깨달음입니다.

**실천적 함의:**
모든 것이 하나라면, 타인을 해치는 것은 자신을 해치는 것입니다. 모든 형태의 황금률은 이 인식에서 자연스럽게 흘러나옵니다.

## II. The Principle of Interconnection | 상호연결의 원리

### 緣起 — All Things Arise Together

**English:**
Nothing exists in isolation. Every phenomenon arises in dependence upon causes and conditions, connected to everything else in an infinite web of relationship.

> "When this exists, that comes to be. With the arising of this, that arises." — Buddha, *Samyutta Nikaya*

> "No man is an island, entire of itself; every man is a piece of the continent, a part of the main." — John Donne

> "A human being is a part of the whole called by us 'universe,' a part limited in time and space. He experiences himself, his thoughts and feelings, as something separated from the rest—a kind of optical delusion of consciousness." — Albert Einstein

> "Heaven, Earth, and I are born together; the ten thousand things and I are one." — Zhuangzi

**Scientific Confirmation:**
- **Ecology**: Every organism depends on its ecosystem
- **Quantum physics**: Entanglement—particles correlated across space
- **Systems theory**: Emergence—wholes greater than parts
- **Neuroscience**: The brain as network; consciousness as integration

**한국어:**
어떤 것도 고립되어 존재하지 않습니다. 모든 현상은 원인과 조건에 의존하여 발생하며, 관계의 무한한 그물 안에서 다른 모든 것과 연결되어 있습니다.

> "이것이 있을 때 저것이 있게 된다. 이것이 일어남으로써 저것이 일어난다." — 부처님

---

## III. The Principle of Impermanence | 무상의 원리

### 諸行無常 — All Things Change

**English:**
Change is the only constant. All conditioned phenomena arise, persist for a time, and pass away. Attachment to the permanent in an impermanent world is the root of suffering.

> "All conditioned things are impermanent. When one perceives this with wisdom, one becomes disenchanted with suffering." — *Dhammapada* 277

> "You cannot step into the same river twice." — Heraclitus

> "To everything there is a season, and a time to every purpose under heaven." — *Ecclesiastes* 3:1

> "The only thing that is constant is change." — Modern proverb, echoing ancient wisdom

**Wisdom Response:**
- **Buddhism**: Non-attachment; embrace impermanence
- **Stoicism**: Focus on what you can control
- **Taoism**: Flow with change like water
- **Existentialism**: Create meaning in the face of finitude
- **Indigenous wisdom**: Cycles of nature; death and rebirth

**한국어:**
변화만이 유일한 상수입니다. 모든 조건 지어진 현상은 일어나고, 잠시 지속하고, 사라집니다. 무상한 세계에서 영구적인 것에 대한 집착이 고통의 뿌리입니다.

> "제행무상. 지혜로 이것을 알면 고에서 벗어난다." — 『법구경』 277

---

## IV. The Principle of Self-Knowledge | 자기 인식의 원리

### 知人者智 自知者明 — Know Thyself

**English:**
The beginning of wisdom is self-knowledge. Before seeking to understand the cosmos, one must understand the one who seeks to understand.

> "Know thyself." (*Gnōthi seauton*) — Inscribed at the Temple of Apollo at Delphi

> "The unexamined life is not worth living." — Socrates

> "He who knows others is wise; he who knows himself is enlightened." — *Tao Te Ching* 33

> "When you know yourselves, then you will be known, and you will understand that you are children of the living Father." — *Gospel of Thomas* 3

> "Tat tvam asi"—"Thou art That." — *Chāndogya Upaniṣad* 6.8.7

**The Journey Inward:**

| Tradition | Path of Self-Knowledge |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Buddhism** | Vipassanā—insight meditation |
| **Hinduism** | Ātma-vicāra—self-inquiry |
| **Sufism** | Muraqaba—contemplation |
| **Christianity** | Contemplative prayer; *examen* |
| **Psychoanalysis** | Making the unconscious conscious |
| **Phenomenology** | Epoché—examining consciousness itself |

**한국어:**
지혜의 시작은 자기 인식입니다. 우주를 이해하고자 하기 전에, 이해하고자 하는 자를 먼저 이해해야 합니다.

> "너 자신을 알라." — 델포이 아폴론 신전에 새겨진 글

> "지인자지 자지자명 (知人者智 自知者明)—타인을 아는 자는 지혜롭고, 자신을 아는 자는 밝다." — 『도덕경』 33

---

## V. The Principle of Transcendence | 초월의 원리

### 超越 — Beyond the Ordinary Self

**English:**
Human beings are not limited to their ordinary ego-consciousness. Every tradition speaks of a higher possibility—liberation, enlightenment, salvation, union with the Divine, self-actualization.

> "The Kingdom of God is within you." — Jesus, *Luke* 17:21

> "Nirvana is the extinction of craving." — Buddha

> "The soul that rises with us... hath had elsewhere its setting, and cometh from afar." — Wordsworth, echoing Plato

> "Die before you die, and find that there is no death." — attributed to Prophet Muhammad

> "What a man can be, he must be. This need we call self-actualization." — Abraham Maslow

**The Goal Across Traditions:**

| Tradition | Ultimate Goal |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Buddhism** | Nirvana—liberation from suffering |
| **Hinduism** | Moksha—freedom from the cycle of rebirth |
| **Christianity** | Theosis—union with God |
| **Islam** | Fanā—annihilation in God |
| **Judaism** | Devekut—cleaving to God |
| **Taoism** | Return to the Tao |
| **Humanism** | Self-actualization; flourishing |

**한국어:**
인간은 일상적 에고-의식에 국한되지 않습니다. 모든 전통은 더 높은 가능성에 대해 말합니다—해탈, 깨달음, 구원, 신과의 합일, 자기 실현.

> "하나님의 나라는 너희 안에 있느니라." — 예수, 『누가복음』 17:21

---

## VI. The Principle of Compassion | 자비의 원리

### 仁愛 — Love as the Highest Virtue

**English:**
Love, compassion, and care for others appear as supreme values across virtually all traditions. The awakened heart naturally responds to the suffering of others.

> "Love your neighbor as yourself." — *Leviticus* 19:18; Jesus

> "Compassion is the wish for all beings to be free from suffering." — Buddhist definition of *karuṇā*

> "A person of ren, wishing to establish oneself, helps others to establish themselves." — Confucius, *Analects* 6:30

> "The best of people are those who bring most benefit to others." — Prophet Muhammad

> "Act in such a way that you treat humanity... never merely as a means, but always at the same time as an end." — Kant

**The Golden Rule Across Traditions:**

| Tradition | Formulation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Confucianism** | 己所不欲 勿施於人—Do not do to others what you would not want done to yourself |
| **Judaism** | Love your neighbor as yourself |
| **Christianity** | Do unto others as you would have them do unto you |
| **Islam** | None of you truly believes until he wishes for his brother what he wishes for himself |
| **Hinduism** | One should never do to another what one regards as injurious to oneself |
| **Buddhism** | Hurt not others in ways that you yourself would find hurtful |
| **Jainism** | One should treat all creatures as one would like to be treated |

**한국어:**
사랑, 자비, 타인에 대한 돌봄은 거의 모든 전통에서 최고의 가치로 나타납니다. 깨어난 마음은 자연스럽게 타인의 고통에 반응합니다.

> "네 이웃을 네 몸과 같이 사랑하라." — 『레위기』 19:18; 예수

---

## VII. The Principle of Practice | 실천의 원리

### 知行合一 — Unity of Knowledge and Action

**English:**
Wisdom is not merely theoretical but must be embodied in practice. Knowing without doing is incomplete; doing without knowing is blind.

> "Not everyone who says to me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of heaven, but only the one who does the will of my Father." — Jesus, *Matthew* 7:21

> "Faith without works is dead." — *James* 2:26

> "To know and not to do is not yet to know." — Wang Yangming (知行合一)

> "Be the change you wish to see in the world." — attributed to Gandhi

> "Philosophers have only interpreted the world; the point is to change it." — Marx

**Forms of Practice:**

| Tradition | Core Practice |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Buddhism** | Meditation, ethical conduct, wisdom |
| **Hinduism** | Yoga, devotion, selfless action |
| **Christianity** | Prayer, love, service |
| **Islam** | Five Pillars—prayer, fasting, charity |
| **Judaism** | Mitzvot—613 commandments |
| **Confucianism** | Ritual, study, self-cultivation |
| **Sikhism** | Naam, seva, simran |

**한국어:**
지혜는 단순히 이론적인 것이 아니라 실천에서 구현되어야 합니다. 행함 없이 아는 것은 불완전하고; 앎 없이 행하는 것은 맹목적입니다.

> "지행합일 (知行合一)—앎과 행함은 하나다." — 왕양명

---

## VIII. The Principle of Balance | 균형의 원리

### 中庸 — The Middle Way

**English:**
Wisdom lies in balance—avoiding extremes, finding the mean, harmonizing opposites. Excess in any direction leads to imbalance and suffering.

> "The Middle Way avoids both extremes—indulgence in sensual pleasures and self-mortification." — Buddha

> "Virtue is the mean between extremes." — Aristotle

> "The Tao moves by returning; the Tao functions by weakness. The ten thousand things are born of being; being is born of non-being." — *Tao Te Ching* 40

> "中庸之道—The Doctrine of the Mean." — Confucian classic

> "Everything in moderation, including moderation." — Oscar Wilde, echoing ancient wisdom

**Dynamic Balance:**
- Yin and Yang in Taoism
- Via media in Christianity
- Middle Path in Buddhism
- Golden Mean in Greek philosophy
- Homeostasis in biology

**한국어:**
지혜는 균형에 있습니다—극단을 피하고, 중용을 찾고, 대립을 조화시킵니다. 어느 방향으로든 과도함은 불균형과 고통으로 이끕니다.

> "중도는 감각적 쾌락에의 탐닉과 자기 학대 양 극단을 피한다." — 부처님

> "중용지도 (中庸之道)." — 유교 고전

---

## IX. The Principle of Humility | 겸손의 원리

### 虛心 — The Empty Cup

**English:**
True wisdom begins with the recognition of one's own ignorance. The wise are humble; the arrogant remain blind.

> "I know that I know nothing." — Socrates

> "The Tao that can be spoken is not the eternal Tao." — Laozi

> "Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven." — Jesus, *Matthew* 5:3

> "The more I learn, the more I realize how much I don't know." — Einstein

> "God cannot fill what is already full. Empty yourself and receive." — Sufi teaching

**The Beginner's Mind:**
- Zen: *Shoshin*—beginner's mind
- Kabbalah: *Bitul*—self-nullification
- Sufism: *Faqr*—spiritual poverty
- Science: Epistemic humility

**한국어:**
참된 지혜는 자신의 무지를 인식하는 것에서 시작됩니다. 현자는 겸손하고; 오만한 자는 맹목적으로 남습니다.

> "나는 내가 아무것도 모른다는 것을 안다." — 소크라테스

> "도가도비상도 (道可道非常道)—말할 수 있는 도는 영원한 도가 아니다." — 노자

---

## X. The Principle of Service | 봉사의 원리

### 弘益人間 — Benefit All Humanity

**English:**
The culmination of wisdom is service to others. Self-realization leads not to isolation but to compassionate action for the benefit of all beings.

> "弘益人間 (Hongik Ingan)—Broadly benefit all humanity." — Korean founding principle (traditionally 2333 BCE)

> "The bodhisattva vows not to enter final Nirvana until all beings are liberated." — Mahāyāna Buddhism

> "I am a servant of the servants of God." — Francis of Assisi

> "The best of people are those who are most useful to others." — Prophet Muhammad

> "Everybody can be great, because anybody can serve." — Martin Luther King Jr.

**Service Across Traditions:**

| Tradition | Form of Service |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Sikhism** | Seva—selfless service |
| **Christianity** | Diakonia—ministry |
| **Judaism** | Tikkun Olam—repair of the world |
| **Islam** | Khidmah—service |
| **Buddhism** | Bodhisattva activity |
| **Hinduism** | Karma Yoga |
| **Secular** | Philanthropy, volunteerism |

**한국어:**
지혜의 정점은 타인에 대한 봉사입니다. 자아 실현은 고립이 아니라 모든 존재의 이익을 위한 자비로운 행동으로 이끕니다.

> "弘益人間 (홍익인간)—널리 인간을 이롭게 하라." — 한국 건국 이념 (전통적으로 기원전 2333년)

## Synthesis | 종합

**English:**
These ten principles are not separate doctrines but facets of a single diamond—the perennial wisdom that has guided humanity across cultures and centuries. They emerge wherever human beings reflect deeply on the nature of existence and the path to a good life.

The principles are:
1. **Unity**—All is one
2. **Interconnection**—All things arise together
3. **Impermanence**—All things change
4. **Self-Knowledge**—Know thyself
5. **Transcendence**—Beyond the ordinary self
6. **Compassion**—Love as the highest virtue
7. **Practice**—Unity of knowledge and action
8. **Balance**—The middle way
9. **Humility**—The empty cup
10. **Service**—Benefit all humanity

Together, they form the **弘益人間** vision: wisdom in service of all.

**한국어:**
이 열 가지 원칙은 분리된 교리가 아니라 하나의 다이아몬드의 면들입니다—문화와 세기를 넘어 인류를 인도해 온 영원한 지혜. 그것들은 인간이 존재의 본성과 좋은 삶으로의 길을 깊이 성찰하는 곳마다 출현합니다.

함께, 그것들은 **弘益人間** 비전을 형성합니다: 모든 것을 위한 봉사 속의 지혜.
