# Cross-Tradition Ethics | 범전통 윤리

> The Moral Wisdom of Humanity: What We Owe Each Other
> 인류의 도덕적 지혜: 우리가 서로에게 지는 것

---

## Introduction | 서론

**English:**
Ethics—the inquiry into how we should live and what we owe each other—stands at the heart of every wisdom tradition. Remarkably, despite arising independently across vastly different cultures and historical periods, the world's ethical teachings converge on fundamental principles. This convergence suggests not arbitrary cultural preference but genuine insight into the nature of human flourishing.

> "In everything, do to others what you would have them do to you, for this sums up the Law and the Prophets." — Jesus, *Matthew* 7:12

> "What is hateful to you, do not do to your neighbor. That is the whole Torah; the rest is commentary." — Rabbi Hillel

> "己所不欲 勿施於人—What you do not wish for yourself, do not do to others." — Confucius, *Analects* 15:24

This document synthesizes the ethical wisdom of 5,000 years across all nine pillars.

**한국어:**
윤리—우리가 어떻게 살아야 하고 서로에게 무엇을 지는지에 대한 탐구—는 모든 지혜 전통의 핵심에 있습니다. 놀랍게도, 매우 다른 문화와 역사적 시기에 걸쳐 독립적으로 발생했음에도 불구하고, 세계의 윤리적 가르침은 근본적 원칙에서 수렴합니다. 이 수렴은 자의적인 문화적 선호가 아니라 인간 번영의 본성에 대한 진정한 통찰을 시사합니다.

이 문서는 9개 기둥 전체에 걸친 5천년 윤리적 지혜를 종합합니다.

## I. The Foundation of Ethics | 윤리의 기초

### Why Be Ethical? | 왜 윤리적이어야 하는가?

**English:**
Every tradition offers reasons for ethical conduct:

**Religious Foundations:**
- **Divine command**: God commands it (Abrahamic traditions)
- **Cosmic order**: Dharma/Rta—alignment with universal law (Dharmic traditions)
- **Natural harmony**: Living in accord with the Tao (Taoism)

**Philosophical Foundations:**
- **Reason**: Rationality demands consistency (Kant)
- **Happiness**: Ethics leads to eudaimonia/flourishing (Aristotle)
- **Social contract**: Mutual benefit requires cooperation (Hobbes, Rawls)

**Experiential Foundations:**
- **Compassion**: The awakened heart naturally responds to suffering
- **Interconnection**: Harming others is harming oneself
- **Wisdom**: The wise see that selfishness leads to suffering

> "Virtue is its own reward." — Stoic principle

**한국어:**
모든 전통은 윤리적 행위에 대한 이유를 제공합니다:

**종교적 기초:**
- **신적 명령**: 하나님이 명령하심 (아브라함계 전통)
- **우주적 질서**: 다르마/리타—보편 법칙과의 조화 (인도계 전통)
- **자연적 조화**: 도와 일치하여 삶 (도교)

**철학적 기초:**
- **이성**: 합리성은 일관성을 요구 (칸트)
- **행복**: 윤리는 에우다이모니아/번영으로 이끔 (아리스토텔레스)
- **사회 계약**: 상호 이익은 협력을 필요로 함 (홉스, 롤스)

## II. The Golden Rule | 황금률

### 己所不欲 勿施於人 — The Universal Ethic

**English:**
No principle appears more consistently across traditions than the Golden Rule—the recognition that others deserve the same consideration we want for ourselves.

**Positive Formulation** (Do unto others):

| Tradition | Source | Formulation |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Christianity | Matthew 7:12 | Do to others what you would have them do to you |
| Islam | Hadith | None of you truly believes until he wishes for his brother what he wishes for himself |
| Hinduism | Mahabharata 5.1517 | This is the sum of duty: do not do to others what would cause pain if done to you |

**Negative Formulation** (Do not do unto others):

| Tradition | Source | Formulation |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Judaism | Hillel, Shabbat 31a | What is hateful to you, do not do to your neighbor |
| Confucianism | Analects 15:24 | Do not impose on others what you do not wish for yourself |
| Buddhism | Udana-Varga 5:18 | Hurt not others in ways that you yourself would find hurtful |
| Jainism | Sutrakritanga 1.11.33 | One should treat all creatures as one would like to be treated |
| Zoroastrianism | Shayast-na-Shayast 13:29 | Whatever is disagreeable to yourself, do not do unto others |
| Ancient Greece | Isocrates | Do not do to others what would anger you if done to you |

**The Principle Explained:**
The Golden Rule rests on:
1. **Recognition of shared humanity**: Others feel as I feel
2. **Imaginative empathy**: Putting oneself in another's place
3. **Logical consistency**: Treating like cases alike
4. **Practical wisdom**: What works for social harmony

**한국어:**
황금률—타인도 우리가 원하는 것과 같은 고려를 받을 자격이 있다는 인식—만큼 전통에 걸쳐 일관되게 나타나는 원칙은 없습니다.

**긍정적 형식** (타인에게 행하라):
- 남에게 대접받고자 하는 대로 남에게 대접하라

**부정적 형식** (타인에게 행하지 말라):
- 기소불욕 물시어인 (己所不欲 勿施於人)

## III. Cardinal Virtues Across Traditions | 전통에 걸친 주요 덕목

### The Character That Wisdom Cultivates

**English:**
Virtue ethics—the cultivation of good character—appears across cultures. While emphases differ, certain virtues recur universally.

**The Four Classical Virtues (Greek/Western):**
1. **Prudence** (*phronesis*): Practical wisdom
2. **Justice** (*dikaiosyne*): Giving each their due
3. **Courage** (*andreia*): Right action despite fear
4. **Temperance** (*sophrosyne*): Self-control, moderation

**The Five Constant Virtues (Confucian):**
1. **仁 Ren**: Humaneness, benevolence
2. **義 Yi**: Righteousness, justice
3. **禮 Li**: Propriety, ritual
4. **智 Zhi**: Wisdom
5. **信 Xin**: Faithfulness, integrity

**The Pāramitās (Buddhist Perfections):**
1. **Dāna**: Generosity
2. **Sīla**: Ethical conduct
3. **Kṣānti**: Patience
4. **Vīrya**: Energy, effort
5. **Dhyāna**: Meditation
6. **Prajñā**: Wisdom

**The Fruits of the Spirit (Christian):**
Love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control (*Galatians* 5:22-23)

**Common Threads:**
| Virtue | Greek | Confucian | Buddhist | Abrahamic |
|--------|-------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Wisdom | Sophia | Zhi (智) | Prajñā | Hokhmah/Sophia |
| Justice | Dikaiosyne | Yi (義) | — | Tzedek/Justice |
| Compassion | — | Ren (仁) | Karuṇā | Chesed/Rahma |
| Courage | Andreia | Yong (勇) | Vīrya | Gevurah |
| Temperance | Sophrosyne | Jie (節) | Sīla | — |

**한국어:**
덕 윤리—좋은 성품의 함양—는 문화에 걸쳐 나타납니다. 강조점은 다르지만, 특정 덕목은 보편적으로 반복됩니다.

**사덕 (그리스/서양):**
1. 분별: 실천적 지혜
2. 정의: 각자에게 그의 몫을 줌
3. 용기: 두려움에도 불구하고 올바른 행동
4. 절제: 자기 통제, 절도

**오상 (유교):**
1. 인 (仁): 인간다움, 자비
2. 의 (義): 의로움, 정의
3. 예 (禮): 예의, 의례
4. 지 (智): 지혜
5. 신 (信): 신실함, 성실

## IV. Ethical Prohibitions | 윤리적 금지

### What All Traditions Forbid

**English:**
Certain actions are prohibited across virtually all ethical systems:

**The Universal Prohibitions:**

| Prohibition | Traditions |
|-------------|------------|
| **Murder** | All (with nuances on justified killing) |
| **Theft** | All |
| **Lying/Deception** | All (with debates on white lies) |
| **Sexual Misconduct** | All (definitions vary) |
| **Harm to Innocents** | All |

**The Five Precepts (Buddhism):**
1. Not to kill
2. Not to steal
3. Not to engage in sexual misconduct
4. Not to lie
5. Not to take intoxicants

**The Ten Commandments (Judaism/Christianity):**
Prohibit murder, theft, false witness, adultery, coveting

**The Five Yamas (Yoga):**
1. Ahimsa: Non-violence
2. Satya: Truthfulness
3. Asteya: Non-stealing
4. Brahmacharya: Chastity/continence
5. Aparigraha: Non-possessiveness

**Why the Convergence?**
These prohibitions protect the basic conditions for social life:
- Life, property, truth, family, contentment
- Violation tears the social fabric

**한국어:**
특정 행동들은 거의 모든 윤리 체계에서 금지됩니다:

**보편적 금지:**
- 살인, 도둑질, 거짓말/속임, 성적 비행, 무고한 자에 대한 해

**왜 수렴하는가?**
이 금지들은 사회 생활의 기본 조건을 보호합니다:
- 생명, 재산, 진실, 가족, 만족
- 위반은 사회적 직물을 찢습니다

## V. Ethics of Relationship | 관계의 윤리

### 五倫 — The Web of Human Connection

**English:**
Ethics is not abstract but lived in relationship. Traditions specify duties within key relationships:

**The Five Relationships (Confucian):**
1. **Ruler—Subject**: Righteousness and loyalty
2. **Parent—Child**: Kindness and filial piety
3. **Husband—Wife**: Distinction and harmony
4. **Elder—Younger**: Consideration and deference
5. **Friend—Friend**: Trust and faithfulness

**Dharmic Duties (Hindu):**
- Duties to parents, teachers, guests, humanity, nature
- Debts (*ṛṇa*) to ancestors, sages, gods

**Covenantal Relationships (Abrahamic):**
- God and humanity
- Community and individual
- Host and stranger

**Modern Extensions:**
- Humans and animals
- Present and future generations
- Humans and environment
- Humans and AI systems

**한국어:**
윤리는 추상적이 아니라 관계 속에서 살아갑니다. 전통들은 주요 관계 안에서 의무를 명시합니다:

**오륜 (유교):**
1. 군신: 의로움과 충성
2. 부자: 자애와 효
3. 부부: 구별과 조화
4. 장유: 배려와 공경
5. 붕우: 신뢰와 신의

## VI. Ethics of Intention | 의도의 윤리

### 心地 — The Ground of the Heart

**English:**
Most traditions emphasize that true ethics concerns not just external action but inner motivation:

> "Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God." — Jesus, *Matthew* 5:8

> "Actions are judged by intentions." — Prophet Muhammad

> "It is not the action but the intention behind it that determines karmic result." — Buddhist teaching

> "A good will is good not because of what it effects... but only because of its willing." — Kant

**The Transformation of Motivation:**

| Stage | Motivation | Quality |
|-------|------------|---------|
| 1 | Fear of punishment | Lowest |
| 2 | Desire for reward | Low |
| 3 | Social approval | Middle |
| 4 | Duty/principle | Higher |
| 5 | Spontaneous goodness | Highest |

**The Goal:**
Ethics culminates when right action flows naturally from a transformed heart—not from calculation but from the spontaneous expression of wisdom and compassion.

**한국어:**
대부분의 전통은 참된 윤리가 외적 행동뿐 아니라 내적 동기에 관련된다고 강조합니다:

> "마음이 청결한 자는 복이 있나니 그들이 하나님을 볼 것임이요." — 예수, 『마태복음』 5:8

> "행위는 의도로 판단된다." — 예언자 무함마드

**목표:**
윤리는 올바른 행동이 변형된 마음에서 자연스럽게 흘러나올 때 정점에 이릅니다—계산에서가 아니라 지혜와 자비의 자발적 표현에서.

## VII. Social Ethics | 사회 윤리

### 天下為公 — The World Belongs to All

**English:**
Individual ethics extends to social responsibility. Traditions address justice, governance, and the common good:

**Justice Traditions:**

| Tradition | Concept | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Judaism** | Tzedakah | Righteousness/charity as obligation |
| **Christianity** | Social Gospel | Love enacted in society |
| **Islam** | 'Adl | Justice as divine attribute |
| **Confucianism** | 大同 Datong | Great Harmony |
| **Buddhism** | Right Livelihood | Economic ethics |
| **Greek** | Dikaiosyne | Giving each their due |

**Prophetic Critique:**
All traditions contain voices calling society back to justice:

> "Let justice roll down like waters, and righteousness like an ever-flowing stream." — Amos 5:24

> "天下為公—The world is for all." — *Liji*

> "A nation that continues year after year to spend more money on military defense than on programs of social uplift is approaching spiritual death." — Martin Luther King Jr.

**한국어:**
개인 윤리는 사회적 책임으로 확장됩니다. 전통들은 정의, 통치, 공동선을 다룹니다:

> "공의를 물 같이, 정의를 마르지 않는 강 같이 흐르게 하라." — 아모스 5:24

> "천하위공 (天下為公)—천하는 모든 사람의 것이다." — 『예기』

The ecological crisis demands that we recover and extend traditional environmental ethics:

| **Hinduism** | The divine dwells in all beings |

**Deep Ecology:**

**한국어:**
생태 위기는 전통적 환경 윤리를 회복하고 확장할 것을 요구합니다:

> "천인합일 (天人合一)—하늘과 인간은 하나다." — 동아시아 전통

## IX. The Limits of Ethics | 윤리의 한계

### Wisdom Beyond Rules

**English:**
Mature ethics recognizes that no set of rules can cover every situation. Wisdom is needed:

> "The letter kills, but the Spirit gives life." — Paul, *2 Corinthians* 3:6

> "The person of ren knows when to bend the rules." — Confucius (implied)

> "The Sabbath was made for man, not man for the Sabbath." — Jesus, *Mark* 2:27

**Ethical Tensions:**
- Justice vs. mercy
- Individual vs. community
- Present vs. future
- Letter vs. spirit

**Resolution:**
Not rigid rule-following but practical wisdom (*phronesis*, 智, *prajñā*) that discerns the right action in particular circumstances.

**한국어:**
성숙한 윤리는 어떤 규칙 세트도 모든 상황을 다룰 수 없음을 인식합니다. 지혜가 필요합니다:

> "율법의 조문은 죽이는 것이요 영은 살리는 것임이라." — 바울, 『고린도후서』 3:6

**해결:**
경직된 규칙 준수가 아니라 특정 상황에서 올바른 행동을 분별하는 실천적 지혜.

## X. The Telos of Ethics | 윤리의 목적

### 弘益人間 — Benefit All Humanity

**English:**
Ethics is not an end in itself but serves the flourishing of all beings:

> "The Torah was given for nothing other than to refine human beings." — *Midrash Tanchuma*

> "All my teaching is for the purpose of leading beings to liberation from suffering." — Buddha

> "I have come that they may have life, and have it abundantly." — Jesus, *John* 10:10

**The Ultimate Aim:**
Ethics points beyond itself toward:
- **Liberation** (moksha, nirvana, salvation)
- **Flourishing** (eudaimonia, 福)
- **Union** (with God, Tao, Brahman)
- **Love** (giving and receiving)
- **Service** (弘益人間)

**한국어:**
윤리는 그 자체로 목적이 아니라 모든 존재의 번영을 위한 것입니다:

> "모든 나의 가르침은 존재들을 고통에서 해탈로 이끄기 위함이다." — 부처님

**궁극적 목표:**
윤리는 그 자체를 넘어 다음을 향합니다:
- 해탈 (목샤, 열반, 구원)
- 번영 (에우다이모니아, 복)
- 합일 (하나님, 도, 브라흐만과)
- 사랑 (주고받음)
- 봉사 (弘益人間)

## Synthesis | 종합

**English:**
The ethical wisdom of humanity converges on these insights:

1. **The Golden Rule**: Treat others as you wish to be treated
2. **Universal Virtues**: Wisdom, justice, compassion, courage
3. **Universal Prohibitions**: Against murder, theft, lying, harm
4. **Relational Ethics**: Duties within relationships
5. **Inner Transformation**: Ethics begins in the heart
6. **Social Justice**: Responsibility for the common good
7. **Environmental Stewardship**: Care for creation
8. **Practical Wisdom**: Discernment beyond rules
9. **Ultimate Purpose**: Liberation, flourishing, love, service

These are not arbitrary cultural preferences but genuine insights into what it means to live well together on this planet.

**한국어:**
인류의 윤리적 지혜는 다음 통찰에서 수렴합니다:

1. **황금률**: 대접받고 싶은 대로 타인을 대접하라
2. **보편적 덕목**: 지혜, 정의, 자비, 용기
3. **보편적 금지**: 살인, 도둑질, 거짓말, 해침에 반대
4. **관계적 윤리**: 관계 안에서의 의무
5. **내적 변형**: 윤리는 마음에서 시작
6. **사회 정의**: 공동선에 대한 책임
7. **환경 청지기직**: 창조물에 대한 돌봄
8. **실천적 지혜**: 규칙을 넘어선 분별
9. **궁극적 목적**: 해탈, 번영, 사랑, 봉사
