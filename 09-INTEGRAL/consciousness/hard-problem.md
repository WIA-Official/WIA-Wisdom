# The Hard Problem of Consciousness | 의식의 난제

> Why Is There Something It Is Like? The Explanatory Gap
> 왜 그런 것 같음이 있는가? 설명적 간극

---

## Overview | 개요

**English:**
The "hard problem of consciousness" (coined by David Chalmers in 1995) asks why and how physical processes in the brain give rise to subjective experience—the felt quality of what it is like to be conscious. Even if we fully explain the brain's functions, why is there "something it is like" to have these experiences?

> "It is undeniable that some organisms are subjects of experience. But the question of how it is that these systems are subjects of experience is perplexing. Why is it that when our cognitive systems engage in visual and auditory information processing, we have visual or auditory experience: the quality of deep blue, the sensation of middle C?" — David Chalmers

This is the central puzzle of consciousness studies.

**한국어:**
"의식의 난제" (1995년 데이비드 찰머스가 만든 용어)는 뇌의 물리적 과정이 왜 그리고 어떻게 주관적 경험을 발생시키는지 묻습니다—의식이 있다는 것이 어떤 것 같은지의 느껴지는 질. 우리가 뇌의 기능을 완전히 설명하더라도, 왜 이러한 경험을 갖는 것이 "그런 것 같음"이 있는가?

이것은 의식 연구의 중심적 수수께끼입니다.

---

## Easy vs. Hard Problems | 쉬운 문제 대 어려운 문제

**English:**
> Chalmers distinguishes:

**Easy Problems** (in principle):
- How does the brain process visual information?
- How does attention work?
- How do we report mental states?

**The Hard Problem:**
- Why is there subjective experience at all?
- Why isn't all processing "in the dark"?

> "Even when we have explained the performance of all the cognitive and behavioral functions... there may still remain a further unanswered question."

**한국어:**
**쉬운 문제** (원칙적으로):
- 뇌는 어떻게 시각 정보를 처리하는가?
- 주의는 어떻게 작용하는가?
- 우리는 정신 상태를 어떻게 보고하는가?

**어려운 문제:**
- 왜 주관적 경험이 전혀 있는가?
- 왜 모든 처리가 "어둠 속에서"가 아닌가?

## Proposed Solutions | 제안된 해결책

**English:**
> Various approaches to the hard problem:

| Approach | Position |
|----------|----------|
| **Physicalism** | Consciousness will eventually be explained by physics |
| **Functionalism** | Consciousness is what the brain does |
| **Dualism** | Mind is non-physical |
| **Panpsychism** | Consciousness is fundamental to all matter |
| **Illusionism** | The "hard" quality is an illusion |
| **Mysterialism** | The problem is beyond human understanding |

**한국어:**
| 접근법 | 입장 |
|-------|-----|
| **물리주의** | 의식은 결국 물리학으로 설명될 것 |
| **기능주의** | 의식은 뇌가 하는 것 |
| **이원론** | 정신은 비물리적 |
| **범심론** | 의식은 모든 물질의 근본 |
| **환상주의** | "어려운" 질은 환상 |
| **신비주의** | 문제는 인간 이해를 넘어섬 |

## Qualia | 감각질

**English:**
> *Qualia* are the subjective, felt qualities of experience:

- The redness of red
- The painfulness of pain
- The taste of coffee

> "What is it like to be a bat?" — Thomas Nagel

Nagel argued that even complete objective knowledge of bat brains wouldn't tell us what echolocation *feels like* to a bat.

**한국어:**
> *감각질*은 경험의 주관적, 느껴지는 질입니다:

- 빨강의 빨강다움
- 고통의 고통스러움
- 커피의 맛

> "박쥐가 되는 것은 어떤 것인가?" — 토마스 네이글

## Wisdom Traditions | 지혜 전통

**English:**
> Traditional perspectives on consciousness:

- **Buddhism**: Mind is primary; investigating consciousness directly (Abhidharma)
- **Vedanta**: Consciousness (Brahman/Atman) is the ultimate reality
- **Western Mysticism**: The soul knows God directly
- **Taoism**: The Tao cannot be captured in concepts

> Perhaps the hard problem arises from trying to explain consciousness objectively when it is intrinsically subjective.

**한국어:**
> 의식에 대한 전통적 관점:

- **불교**: 마음이 근본적; 의식을 직접 탐구 (아비담마)
- **베단타**: 의식 (브라흐만/아트만)이 궁극적 실재
- **서양 신비주의**: 영혼이 하나님을 직접 앎
- **도교**: 도는 개념으로 포착될 수 없음
