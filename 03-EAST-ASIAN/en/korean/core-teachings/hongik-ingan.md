# Hongik Ingan (弘益人間) - Benefit All Humanity | 홍익인간 - 널리 인간을 이롭게 하라

> The founding principle of Korean civilization and the guiding philosophy of WIA-Wisdom
> 한국 문명의 건국 이념이자 WIA-Wisdom의 지도 철학

---

## Overview | 개요

Hongik Ingan (弘益人間, Korean: 홍익인간) is an ancient Korean philosophy meaning "to broadly benefit humanity" or "to benefit all people in the world." According to the *Samguk Yusa* (三國遺事, Records of the Three Kingdoms), this principle was given by Hwanin (the Lord of Heaven) to his son Hwanung when sending him to Earth, establishing it as the founding ideal of Gojoseon, Korea's first kingdom, in 2333 BCE.

The phrase breaks down as:
- **弘 (hong)**: Broadly, greatly, to expand
- **益 (ik)**: Benefit, profit, help
- **人間 (ingan)**: The human world, human society (not "human being" as in modern Korean)

Unlike religious commandments or abstract principles, Hongik Ingan is a practical directive: make the world better for everyone. It has been formally adopted as the fundamental principle of Korean education since 1949 and serves as the philosophical foundation of the WIA-Wisdom project.

홍익인간(弘益人間)은 "인간 세상을 널리 이롭게 하다"를 의미하는 한국의 고대 철학입니다. 삼국유사(三國遺事)에 따르면, 이 원칙은 환인(천제)이 아들 환웅을 지상으로 보낼 때 주어진 것으로, 기원전 2333년 한국 최초의 국가인 고조선의 건국 이상으로 확립되었습니다.
종교적 계명이나 추상적 원리와 달리, 홍익인간은 실천적 지침입니다: 모든 사람을 위해 세상을 더 좋게 만들어라. 1949년 이래 한국 교육의 근본 원리로 공식 채택되었으며, WIA-Wisdom 프로젝트의 철학적 기반이 됩니다.

## The Dangun Myth | 단군신화
### Original Text | 원문
> **昔有桓因...庶子桓雄...數意天下貪求人世。父知子意，下視三危太伯，可以弘益人間。**
>
>
> — *Samguk Yusa* (1281 CE), citing *Gogi* (ancient record)
### The Narrative | 이야기
Hwanung descended with 3,000 followers, bringing wind, rain, and cloud spirits. He established a sacred city under the divine birch tree at the summit of Taebaek Mountain. There, he taught laws, agriculture, medicine, and moral principles—all in service of benefiting humanity.

From the union of Hwanung with Ungnyeo (a bear transformed into a woman), Dangun Wanggeom was born. Dangun founded Gojoseon in 2333 BCE with Hongik Ingan as its guiding principle.

## Philosophical Analysis | 철학적 분석
### 弘 (Hong) - Broadly | 홍 - 널리
"Hong" (弘) means not just "widely" but "greatly" and "expansively." It opposes exclusivity, inequality, and monopoly. Benefit is not for one group but for all—transcending tribe, nation, race, and species.

### 益 (Ik) - Benefit | 익 - 이롭게
"Ik" (益) means to benefit, help, or make prosperous. It implies active contribution, not passive goodwill. The interpretation extends to "make happy"—comprehensive wellbeing including material and spiritual dimensions.

### 人間 (Ingan) - Human World | 인간 - 인간 세상
In classical Chinese and Korean, "ingan" (人間) means "among people" or "the human world"—not "a human being" as in modern Korean. The character 間 (gan/between) indicates the space between people: human society, relationships, civilization. Hongik Ingan is thus about benefiting the entire fabric of human existence.

## The Three Powers | 천지인 삼재(三才)
The Dangun myth embeds the cosmological principle of *Samjae* (三才): Heaven (天), Earth (地), and Humanity (人).
- Hwanung represents Heaven descending
- Ungnyeo represents Earth ascending
- Dangun is their union—Humanity, the mediator between Heaven and Earth

Hongik Ingan is the purpose of this cosmic union: Heaven and Earth come together to benefit the human world.

## Modern Application | 현대적 적용
### Korean Education Law (1949) | 대한민국 교육법
> **교육은 홍익인간의 이념 아래 모든 국민으로 하여금 인격을 완성하고, 자주적 생활능력과 공민으로서의 자질을 구유하게 하여, 민주국가 발전에 봉사하며 인류공영의 이상 실현에 기여하게 함을 목적으로 한다.**
> *Education, under the ideal of Hongik Ingan, aims to have all citizens perfect their character, acquire abilities for independent living and civic qualification, serve the development of a democratic nation, and contribute to the realization of the ideal of co-prosperity for all humanity.*
### Why This Matters | 왜 중요한가
Hongik Ingan is notable because it:
1. **Predates organized religion** - Emerges before Buddhism, Christianity, or Islam reached Korea
2. **Is universally inclusive** - Benefits all humanity, not just Koreans or believers
3. **Is actively practical** - A directive to do, not just believe
4. **Unifies with global values** - Aligns with human rights, democracy, sustainability

Scholars note it parallels the spirit of:
- Christian *agape* (unconditional love)
- Buddhist *metta* (loving-kindness)
- Confucian *ren* (benevolence)
- Ubuntu (I am because we are)
