# Xunzi (荀子 Xúnzǐ) | 순자

> Human Nature Is Evil: Ritual, Learning, and the Cultivation of Goodness

---

## Overview | 개요

Xunzi (荀子, c. 310–c. 235 BCE), born Xun Kuang (荀況), was a Chinese Confucian philosopher who, along with Mencius, was one of the most influential interpreters of Confucius. Unlike Mencius, who argued that human nature is inherently good, Xunzi famously maintained that "human nature is evil" (*xìng è*, 性惡)—that our inborn tendencies incline us toward selfishness and conflict.

> "Human nature is evil; goodness is the result of conscious activity." For Xunzi, humans become good through education, ritual, and deliberate cultivation—not by following their natural inclinations.

His emphasis on ritual (*li*), learning, and social institutions as means of transformation had profound influence on Chinese legal philosophy and Confucian tradition.

> "인간 본성은 악하다; 선함은 의식적 활동의 결과이다." 순자에게 인간은 자연적 성향을 따르는 것이 아니라 교육, 예, 의도적 함양을 통해 선해집니다.
---
## Human Nature Is Evil | 인간 본성은 악하다
> "The nature of man is evil; his goodness is acquired." Xunzi argued that left to their natural inclinations, humans would pursue selfish desires leading to conflict and chaos.

> "Now the nature of man is such that he is born with a love of profit. If he follows this, contention and strife will arise and courtesy and deference will perish."

Key arguments:
- Natural desires lead to conflict
- Without restraint, society would collapse
- Goodness requires conscious effort
- Morality is not innate but learned

> "인간의 본성은 악하다; 그의 선함은 후천적이다." 순자는 자연적 성향에 맡겨두면 인간은 갈등과 혼란으로 이끄는 이기적 욕망을 추구할 것이라고 주장했습니다.
---
## The Role of Ritual (禮 Lǐ) | 예의 역할
> For Xunzi, ritual (*li*) is the crucial means by which human beings transform their evil nature into goodness. Ritual provides the structure that channels and refines our raw desires.

> "Ritual is that whereby Heaven and Earth unite, whereby the sun and moon shine, whereby the four seasons proceed in order."

Functions of ritual:
- **Restraint**: Limits destructive impulses
- **Refinement**: Cultivates proper emotions
- **Social Order**: Creates harmonious community
- **Self-Transformation**: Shapes character

> 순자에게 예 (*li*)는 인간이 악한 본성을 선함으로 변형시키는 결정적인 수단입니다. 예는 우리의 원초적 욕망을 안내하고 다듬는 구조를 제공합니다.
---
## Xunzi vs. Mencius | 순자 vs. 맹자
> The debate between Xunzi and Mencius represents one of the great philosophical disputes in Chinese history.

| Xunzi | Mencius |
|-------|---------|
| Human nature is evil | Human nature is good |
| Goodness is acquired | Goodness is innate |
| Ritual transforms | Ritual expresses |
| External cultivation | Internal development |
| Crooked wood metaphor | Sprout metaphor |

> For Mencius, we need cultivation to prevent falling into evil; for Xunzi, we need cultivation to become good.

> 순자와 맹자 사이의 논쟁은 중국 역사에서 가장 위대한 철학적 논쟁 중 하나를 대표합니다.
| 순자 | 맹자 |
|------|------|
| 인간 본성은 악하다 | 인간 본성은 선하다 |
| 선함은 후천적 | 선함은 선천적 |
| 예는 변형시킴 | 예는 표현함 |
| 외적 함양 | 내적 발전 |
| 굽은 나무 은유 | 새싹 은유 |

## The Importance of Learning | 배움의 중요성
> Xunzi emphasized that learning (*xue*, 學) is essential for moral development. The *Xunzi* opens with an essay on "Encouraging Learning."

> "Learning should never cease... The gentleman learns broadly and examines himself daily, then his knowledge will be clear and his conduct without fault."

Types of learning:
- Classical texts and ritual
- Music and proper conduct
- Association with worthy teachers
- Self-examination and reflection

> 순자는 배움 (*학*, 學)이 도덕적 발달에 필수적이라고 강조했습니다. 『순자』는 "권학"에 대한 논문으로 시작합니다.
> "배움은 결코 중단되어서는 안 된다... 군자는 널리 배우고 매일 자신을 살피면, 그의 지식은 명확하고 그의 행실은 과오가 없을 것이다."
---
## Influence on Legalism | 법가에 대한 영향
> Xunzi's students included Han Feizi and Li Si, founders of Legalism. His emphasis on external institutions and human selfishness influenced Legalist thought, though Xunzi remained committed to Confucian moral ideals.

> The difference: Legalists used laws and punishments, while Xunzi advocated ritual and moral education.

> 순자의 학생에는 법가의 창시자인 한비자와 이사가 포함되었습니다. 외적 제도와 인간의 이기심에 대한 그의 강조는 법가 사상에 영향을 미쳤지만, 순자는 유가적 도덕 이상에 헌신했습니다.
> 차이점: 법가는 법과 형벌을 사용했고, 순자는 예와 도덕 교육을 옹호했습니다.
