# Matsuri (Shinto Festivals) | 마츠리 (祭り)

> Sacred Celebrations Honoring the Kami

---

## Overview | 개요

Matsuri (Japanese: 祭り, "festival") refers to any of a wide variety of civil and religious ceremonies in Japan; more particularly, the shrine festivals of Shintō. In Japanese, matsuru means "to honor" or "to celebrate the gods"—it is therefore a feast to thank or pray to the kami (Shinto deities).

It is estimated that there are between 100,000 and 300,000 festivals across Japan, generating an annual economic impact of 530 billion yen. As of 2024, 33 festivals have been registered as UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage.

---
## Connection to Kami | 카미와의 연결

> "The origin of the word matsuri is related to the kami (神, Shinto deities); there are theories that the word is derived from 'matsu' meaning 'to wait (for the kami to descend)', or 'matsurau' meaning 'to obey the kami.'"

### Religious Purpose:
> "Shintoism, an ancient and indigenous religion of Japan, plays a crucial role in shaping the cultural landscape of the nation. Rooted in the veneration of kami—spirits or gods associated with natural elements and ancestors—Shinto is more than just a belief system; it is a way of life."

> "마츠리라는 단어의 기원은 카미(神, 신토 신들)와 관련이 있습니다; '기다리다(카미가 내려오기를)'를 의미하는 '마츠'나 '카미에게 순종하다'를 의미하는 '마츠라우'에서 유래했다는 이론이 있습니다."

## Structure of a Matsuri | 마츠리의 구조

> "A matsuri generally falls into two parts: the solemn ritual of worship, followed by a joyous celebration."

### The Ritual Sequence:
| Stage | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **Purification** | Bathing (misogi), preferably in salt water |
| **Offerings** | Food offerings (shinsen) presented |
| **Prayers** | Norito recited by priests |
| **Individual worship** | Tamagushi offerings |
| **Sacred arts** | Gagaku (music) and bugaku (dance) |
| **Withdrawal** | Kami respectfully asked to retire |

> "마츠리는 일반적으로 두 부분으로 나뉩니다: 엄숙한 예배 의식, 그 다음 기쁜 축하."
| 단계 | 행동 |
|-----|-----|
| **정화** | 목욕(미소기), 소금물 선호 |
| **봉헌** | 음식 봉헌(신센) 제시 |
| **기도** | 신관이 노리토 낭송 |
| **개인 예배** | 타마구시 봉헌 |
| **신성한 예술** | 가가쿠(음악)와 부가쿠(춤) |

## The Mikoshi | 미코시

> "An important element of Japanese festivals are processions, in which the local shrine's kami is carried through the town in mikoshi (palanquins). It is the only time of the year when the kami leaves the shrine to be carried around town."

> "Mikoshi is the most important element of matsuri. Literally 'divine palanquin,' the mikoshi symbolically welcomes 'the spirit of the kami' of the shrine and is carried through the streets of a neighborhood during matsuri."

### Significance:
- Kami travels among the people
- Community participation
- Blessing of the neighborhood
- Renewal of the bond between humans and kami

> "일본 축제의 중요한 요소는 지역 신사의 카미가 미코시(가마)에 실려 마을을 통과하는 행렬입니다. 카미가 마을을 돌아다니기 위해 신사를 떠나는 일년 중 유일한 시간입니다."

## Seasonal Festivals | 계절 축제

> "In agricultural festivals, different ceremonies are held in each of the four seasons."

| Season | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Spring** | Pray for good harvest |
| **Summer** | Protection from pests and storms |
| **Autumn** | Gratitude for harvest |
| **Winter** | Prayer for new year's harvest |

| 계절 | 목적 |
|-----|-----|
| **봄** | 풍작을 위해 기도 |
| **여름** | 해충과 폭풍으로부터 보호 |
| **가을** | 수확에 대한 감사 |
| **겨울** | 새해 수확을 위한 기도 |
