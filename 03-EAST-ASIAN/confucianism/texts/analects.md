# The Analects | 논어 (論語)

> Collected Sayings of Confucius
> 공자의 말씀 모음

---

## Overview | 개요

**English:**
The Analects (論語, Lúnyǔ, "Selected Sayings") is a collection of sayings and ideas attributed to the Chinese philosopher Confucius (551-479 BCE) and his disciples. Compiled by his followers after his death, it remains the most influential source of Confucian philosophy and has shaped East Asian civilization for over two millennia.

The text presents Confucius's ethical vision through conversations, aphorisms, and anecdotes. At its core is the ideal of cultivating virtue to become a junzi (exemplary person) who can bring harmony to society.

**한국어:**
논어(論語)는 중국 철학자 공자(기원전 551-479)와 그의 제자들에게 귀속되는 말씀과 생각의 모음입니다. 그의 사후 제자들에 의해 편찬되었으며, 유교 철학의 가장 영향력 있는 원천으로 남아 2천 년 이상 동아시아 문명을 형성해왔습니다.

텍스트는 대화, 격언, 일화를 통해 공자의 윤리적 비전을 제시합니다. 핵심은 사회에 조화를 가져올 수 있는 군자(모범적 인격)가 되기 위해 덕을 함양하는 이상입니다.

## Ren (仁) - Benevolence | 인 (仁) - 인자함

**English:**

> "The Ren is considered to be the nucleus of all the teachings of Confucius. This word consists of multiple meanings such as benevolence, humanity, human-heartedness, goodness, or love."

### The Character:
> "The character for ren is composed of two graphic elements, one representing a human being and the other representing the number two."

This suggests that ren is fundamentally relational—it exists between people.

### Confucius's Innovation:
> "Confucius was the first to conceive of ren as a general virtue. Prior to Confucius, Chinese tradition did not have a general, universal virtue under which all particular virtues could be subsumed."

### In Practice:
> "What you do not wish for yourself, do not do to others." (己所不欲，勿施於人)

**한국어:**

> "인(仁)은 공자 모든 가르침의 핵심으로 여겨집니다. 이 단어는 인자함, 인간성, 인정, 선, 사랑과 같은 다양한 의미를 담고 있습니다."

### 글자:
> "인(仁) 자는 두 도형 요소로 구성되며, 하나는 인간을, 다른 하나는 숫자 이(二)를 나타냅니다."

## Li (禮) - Ritual Propriety | 예 (禮) - 예절

**English:**

> "Confucius broadened the concept of li beyond mere rituals to include societal behaviors, infusing them with a sacred essence."

### The Connection:
> "Li should be fundamentally based on Ren. Authentic Li should rightly conform to the notion of Ren which aims to achieve moral and social order."

### What Li Includes:
- Religious rituals
- Social etiquette
- Political ceremonies
- Family customs
- Daily courtesy

### Purpose:
Li creates the framework for expressing virtue in social contexts, making the inner quality of ren visible in outward behavior.

**한국어:**

> "공자는 예(禮)의 개념을 단순한 의례를 넘어 사회적 행동을 포함하도록 확장하여, 신성한 본질을 불어넣었습니다."

### 연결:
> "예(禮)는 근본적으로 인(仁)에 기초해야 합니다. 진정한 예는 도덕적, 사회적 질서를 달성하려는 인의 개념에 올바르게 부합해야 합니다."

## Junzi (君子) - The Exemplary Person | 군자 (君子)

**English:**

> "The junzi is a Chinese philosophical term often translated as 'gentleman' or 'superior person.' Junzi, which literally means 'son of a lord,' was redefined by Confucius to describe a person of noble character and ethical virtue."

### Key Features:
> "Confucius created the model of junzi, which may be achieved by any individual through the discipline of one's mind and actions."

Not birth but cultivation determines the junzi.

### The Five Virtues:

| Virtue | Chinese | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Ren** | 仁 | Benevolence |
| **Yi** | 義 | Righteousness |
| **Li** | 禮 | Ritual propriety |
| **Zhi** | 智 | Wisdom |
| **Xin** | 信 | Trustworthiness |

### The Connection to Ren:
> "Ren is fundamental to become a junzi. Exemplary persons do not take leave of their authoritative conduct (ren) even for the space of a meal."

**한국어:**

> "군자는 종종 '신사' 또는 '우월한 사람'으로 번역되는 중국 철학 용어입니다. 문자 그대로 '군주의 아들'을 의미하는 군자는 공자에 의해 고귀한 인격과 윤리적 덕의 인격을 묘사하도록 재정의되었습니다."

## Famous Passages | 유명한 구절

**English:**

### On Learning:
> "Is it not pleasant to learn and practice what you have learned?" (學而時習之，不亦說乎)

### On Self-Cultivation:
> "At fifteen, I set my heart on learning. At thirty, I was established. At forty, I had no doubts. At fifty, I knew the mandate of Heaven." (十五而志於學，三十而立，四十而不惑，五十而知天命)

### On Government:
> "If you lead with virtue and regulate with ritual, the people will have a sense of shame and will order themselves." (道之以德，齊之以禮，有恥且格)

### On the Golden Rule:
> "What you do not wish for yourself, do not do to others." (己所不欲，勿施於人)

**한국어:**

### 배움에 대해:
> "배우고 때때로 익히면 또한 기쁘지 아니한가?"

### 자기수양에 대해:
> "나는 열다섯에 배움에 뜻을 두었고, 서른에 섰고, 마흔에 의혹이 없었고, 쉰에 천명을 알았다."
