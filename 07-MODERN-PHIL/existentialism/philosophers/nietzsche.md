# Friedrich Nietzsche | 프리드리히 니체

> The Philosopher of Life-Affirmation
> 삶 긍정의 철학자

---

## Overview | 개요

**English:**
Friedrich Nietzsche (1844-1900) was a German philosopher whose work has profoundly influenced modern thought. His critiques of religion, morality, and philosophy, along with his concepts of the will to power, eternal recurrence, and the Übermensch, challenged Western civilization's fundamental assumptions.

Writing in an aphoristic and literary style, Nietzsche's philosophy resists systematic summary. Yet his call to create new values, embrace life fully, and become who you truly are continues to resonate across philosophy, psychology, literature, and art.

**한국어:**
프리드리히 니체(1844-1900)는 현대 사상에 깊은 영향을 미친 독일 철학자입니다. 종교, 도덕, 철학에 대한 비판과 함께 권력 의지, 영원 회귀, 위버멘쉬 개념으로 서양 문명의 근본 가정에 도전했습니다.

격언적이고 문학적인 스타일로 글을 쓴 니체의 철학은 체계적 요약에 저항합니다. 그러나 새로운 가치를 창조하고, 삶을 완전히 포용하며, 진정한 자신이 되라는 그의 부름은 철학, 심리학, 문학, 예술 전반에 계속 공명합니다.

## Thus Spoke Zarathustra | 차라투스트라는 이렇게 말했다

**English:**

> "*Thus Spoke Zarathustra: A Book for All and None* is Nietzsche's most famous work. It deals with subjects ranging from metaphysics to war to perspectivism, and began or elaborated on many of his core ideas such as the Übermensch, the death of God, the will to power, and eternal recurrence."

### The Work:
> "*Thus Spoke Zarathustra* stands alone among Nietzsche's published works as a poetic and narrative rendering of his most sophisticated ideas. It is the first place where Nietzsche proposes his theory of will to power."

### The Prophet:
The book follows Zarathustra, a prophet who descends from his mountain solitude to teach humanity about the coming of the Übermensch and the need to create new values.

**한국어:**

> "*차라투스트라는 이렇게 말했다: 모든 이와 아무도를 위한 책*은 니체의 가장 유명한 작품입니다. 형이상학에서 전쟁, 관점주의에 이르는 주제를 다루며, 위버멘쉬, 신의 죽음, 권력 의지, 영원 회귀와 같은 핵심 사상을 시작하거나 발전시켰습니다."

## The Will to Power | 권력 의지

**English:**

> "The will to power is one of the most fundamental concepts in Nietzsche's philosophy. It is also one of his most complex concepts as it was never systematically defined."

### What It Means:
> "Nietzsche was fundamentally concerned with commanding or exercising power over oneself, not over others. The will to power is a dynamic force in continuous becoming and striving, manifesting itself in the encounter with obstacles."

### Not Domination:
The will to power is not about dominating others but about:
- Self-overcoming
- Creative expression
- Growth and expansion
- Mastery of oneself

### Schopenhauer's Influence:
> "Nietzsche's thinking was significantly influenced by Arthur Schopenhauer, who emphasised will to live. Nietzsche emphasised *Wille zur Macht*, or will to power."

**한국어:**

> "권력 의지는 니체 철학에서 가장 근본적인 개념 중 하나입니다. 체계적으로 정의된 적이 없어 가장 복잡한 개념이기도 합니다."

### 의미:
> "니체는 근본적으로 타인이 아닌 자신에 대한 지배나 권력 행사에 관심이 있었습니다. 권력 의지는 계속적인 생성과 노력 속에서 장애물과의 만남에서 나타나는 역동적 힘입니다."

## Eternal Recurrence | 영원 회귀

**English:**

> "Eternal return is a philosophical concept which states that time repeats itself in an infinite loop, and that exactly the same events will continue to occur in exactly the same way, over and over again, for eternity."

### The Test:
> "Nietzsche's eternal recurrence is a litmus test for an individual's capacity to affirm life. Your reaction to the prospect of living every single moment of your life over and over again in sequence is, for Nietzsche, a crucial measure of your ability to become who you truly are."

### From Horror to Joy:
> "Zarathustra is initially struck with horror at the thought that all things must recur eternally; ultimately, however, he overcomes his aversion to eternal return and embraces it as his most fervent desire."

### The Fundamental Idea:
> "In *Ecce Homo*, Nietzsche states: 'The fundamental idea of the work, the Eternal Recurrence, the highest formula of a Yes-saying to life that can ever be attained, was first conceived in the month of August 1881.'"

**한국어:**

> "영원 회귀는 시간이 무한 루프로 반복되며, 정확히 같은 사건이 정확히 같은 방식으로 영원히 계속 일어난다는 철학적 개념입니다."

### 시험:
> "니체의 영원 회귀는 삶을 긍정하는 개인의 능력에 대한 리트머스 테스트입니다. 삶의 모든 순간을 순서대로 계속 반복해서 사는 전망에 대한 반응이 니체에게는 진정한 자신이 되는 능력의 중요한 척도입니다."

## Key Concepts | 핵심 개념

**English:**

### The Übermensch:
The "Overman" or "Superman"—not a biological superior but one who creates new values and overcomes the human condition.

### Death of God:
> "God is dead"—not a celebration but a diagnosis of the crisis of meaning in Western civilization.

### Master and Slave Morality:
Nietzsche distinguished between:
- **Master morality**: Values strength, nobility, pride
- **Slave morality**: Values humility, sympathy, pity

### Amor Fati:
"Love of fate"—embracing everything that happens, including suffering.

### Perspectivism:
There is no objective truth—only interpretations from different perspectives.

**한국어:**

### 위버멘쉬:
"초인"—생물학적 우월자가 아니라 새로운 가치를 창조하고 인간 조건을 초월하는 자.

### 신의 죽음:
> "신은 죽었다"—축하가 아닌 서양 문명의 의미 위기에 대한 진단.

### 아모르 파티:
"운명에 대한 사랑"—고통을 포함해 일어나는 모든 것을 포용.
