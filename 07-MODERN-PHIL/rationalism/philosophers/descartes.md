# René Descartes | 르네 데카르트

> The Father of Modern Philosophy
> 근대 철학의 아버지

---

## Overview | 개요

**English:**
René Descartes (1596-1650) was a French philosopher, mathematician, and scientist who is widely considered the father of modern philosophy. His method of systematic doubt and his famous proposition "Cogito, ergo sum" ("I think, therefore I am") laid the foundation for rationalism, a philosophical system that emphasizes the role of reason in obtaining knowledge.

Because he formulated the first modern version of mind-body dualism and promoted the development of a new science grounded in observation and experiment, he is generally regarded as the founder of modern philosophy.

**한국어:**
르네 데카르트(1596-1650)는 프랑스 철학자, 수학자, 과학자로 널리 근대 철학의 아버지로 여겨집니다. 그의 체계적 의심의 방법과 유명한 명제 "코기토, 에르고 숨"("나는 생각한다, 고로 존재한다")은 지식 획득에서 이성의 역할을 강조하는 철학 체계인 합리주의의 기초를 놓았습니다.

그는 심신 이원론의 첫 근대 버전을 공식화하고 관찰과 실험에 기반한 새로운 과학의 발전을 촉진했기 때문에 일반적으로 근대 철학의 창시자로 간주됩니다.

## Cogito Ergo Sum | 코기토 에르고 숨

**English:**

> "'Cogito, ergo sum,' usually translated as 'I think, therefore I am,' is the 'first principle' of René Descartes' philosophy. He originally published it in French as 'je pense, donc je suis' in his 1637 *Discourse on the Method*."

### The Discovery:
> "Although at this stage there is seemingly no belief about which he cannot entertain doubt, Descartes finds certainty in the intuition that, when he is thinking—even if he is being deceived—he must exist."

### Not an Argument:
> "In the *Discourse*, Descartes expresses this intuition in the dictum 'I think, therefore I am'; but because 'therefore' suggests that the intuition is an argument—though it is not—in the *Meditations* he says merely, 'I think, I am' ('Cogito, sum')."

> "The cogito is a logically self-evident truth that also gives intuitively certain knowledge of a particular thing's existence—that is, one's self."

**한국어:**

> "'코기토, 에르고 숨'은 보통 '나는 생각한다, 고로 존재한다'로 번역되며, 르네 데카르트 철학의 '제1원리'입니다."

### 발견:
> "이 단계에서 의심할 수 없는 믿음이 없는 것처럼 보이지만, 데카르트는 생각할 때—속임을 당하더라도—자신이 존재해야 한다는 직관에서 확실성을 찾습니다."

## The Meditations | 성찰

**English:**

> "*Meditations on First Philosophy*, first published in 1641, set forth 'a new metaphysical dualism based on a radical distinction between mind and matter (or mind and body) and established a rational foundation for human knowledge.'"

### Structure:
| Meditation | Focus |
|------------|-------|
| **First** | Method of doubt |
| **Second** | Cogito; nature of mind |
| **Third** | Existence of God |
| **Fourth** | Truth and error |
| **Fifth** | Essence of material things |
| **Sixth** | Mind-body distinction |

> "Descartes constructed the Meditations so as to secure this process of withdrawal from the senses in Meditation I. Meditation II discovers an initial truth, the cogito."

**한국어:**

| 성찰 | 초점 |
|-----|-----|
| **첫 번째** | 의심의 방법 |
| **두 번째** | 코기토; 정신의 본성 |
| **세 번째** | 하나님의 존재 |
| **네 번째** | 진리와 오류 |
| **다섯 번째** | 물질적 사물의 본질 |
| **여섯 번째** | 심신 구분 |

## Mind-Body Dualism | 심신 이원론

**English:**

> "Descartes developed a metaphysical dualism that distinguishes radically between mind, the essence of which is thinking, and matter, the essence of which is extension in three dimensions."

> "The theory on the dualism of mind and body is Descartes's signature doctrine and permeates other theories he advanced. Known as Cartesian dualism (or mind–body dualism), his theory on the separation between the mind and the body went on to influence subsequent Western philosophies."

### The Distinction:
| Mind (Res Cogitans) | Body (Res Extensa) |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Thinking substance | Extended substance |
| Non-spatial | Spatial |
| Indivisible | Divisible |
| Private | Public |

**한국어:**

> "데카르트는 본질이 사고인 정신과 본질이 3차원 확장인 물질 사이를 급진적으로 구별하는 형이상학적 이원론을 발전시켰습니다."

| 정신 (Res Cogitans) | 몸 (Res Extensa) |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| 사고 실체 | 연장 실체 |
| 비공간적 | 공간적 |
| 나눌 수 없음 | 나눌 수 있음 |

## Rationalism | 합리주의

**English:**

> "His method of systematic doubt and his famous proposition 'Cogito, ergo sum' laid the foundation for rationalism, a philosophical system that emphasizes the role of reason in obtaining knowledge."

### Key Features:
- Knowledge through reason alone
- Innate ideas exist
- Mathematical certainty as model
- Skepticism as method
- Clear and distinct ideas as criterion

> "The ontological argument for God's existence is at the heart of Descartes's rationalism, for it establishes certain knowledge about an existing thing solely on the basis of reasoning from innate ideas, with no help from sensory experience."

**한국어:**

### 핵심 특징:
- 이성만을 통한 지식
- 본유 관념의 존재
- 모델로서의 수학적 확실성
- 방법으로서의 회의주의
- 기준으로서의 명석판명한 관념
