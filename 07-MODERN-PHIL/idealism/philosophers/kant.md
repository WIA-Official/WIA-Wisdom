# Immanuel Kant | 임마누엘 칸트

> The Central Figure in Modern Philosophy
> 현대 철학의 중심 인물

---

## Overview | 개요

**English:**
Immanuel Kant (1724–1804) is the central figure in modern philosophy. He synthesized early modern rationalism and empiricism, set the terms for much of nineteenth and twentieth century philosophy, and continues to exercise significant influence today in metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, political philosophy, aesthetics, and other fields.

His three Critiques—the Critique of Pure Reason (1781), the Critique of Practical Reason (1788), and the Critique of the Power of Judgment (1790)—revolutionized philosophy by arguing that human autonomy is fundamental: the human understanding is the source of nature's laws, and human reason gives itself the moral law.

**한국어:**
임마누엘 칸트(1724–1804)는 현대 철학의 중심 인물입니다. 그는 초기 근대의 합리주의와 경험주의를 종합하고, 19세기와 20세기 철학의 많은 부분의 용어를 설정했으며, 오늘날에도 형이상학, 인식론, 윤리학, 정치철학, 미학 및 기타 분야에서 중요한 영향력을 행사하고 있습니다.

세 비판서—순수이성비판(1781), 실천이성비판(1788), 판단력비판(1790)—은 인간 자율성이 근본적이라고 주장하여 철학을 혁명화했습니다.

## The Categorical Imperative | 정언명령

**English:**

### Definition:
> "The categorical imperative is the central philosophical concept in the deontological moral philosophy of Immanuel Kant. It is a way of evaluating motivations for action."

### The Most Famous Formulation:
> "Act only according to that maxim whereby you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law."

### Key Features:
> "It is categorical in virtue of applying to us unconditionally, or simply because we possess rational wills, without reference to any ends that we might or might not have."

### Distinction from Hypothetical Imperatives:
| Type | Basis | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Hypothetical** | Conditional on desires | "If you want X, do Y" |
| **Categorical** | Unconditional duty | "Do Y, regardless of desires" |

**한국어:**

### 정의:
> "정언명령은 임마누엘 칸트의 의무론적 도덕 철학의 중심 철학적 개념입니다. 행위의 동기를 평가하는 방법입니다."

### 가장 유명한 공식화:
> "네 의지의 준칙이 항상 동시에 보편적 법칙의 원리로서 타당할 수 있도록 행위하라."

## Formulations | 공식화

**English:**

### Three Main Formulations:

| Formulation | Statement |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Universal Law** | Act only on maxims you could will to be universal laws |
| **Humanity** | Treat humanity, whether in yourself or others, always as an end and never merely as a means |
| **Kingdom of Ends** | Act as if you were a legislating member in a universal kingdom of ends |

> "Kant articulated the categorical imperative in two primary forms: first, that individuals should act according to maxims they would wish to become universal laws; and second, that people should treat others as ends in themselves and never merely as means to an end."

**한국어:**

### 세 가지 주요 공식화:

| 공식화 | 진술 |
|-------|-----|
| **보편 법칙** | 보편적 법칙이 될 수 있는 준칙에 따라서만 행위하라 |
| **인간성** | 인간성을 항상 목적으로 대우하고 수단으로만 대우하지 말라 |
| **목적의 왕국** | 보편적 목적의 왕국에서 입법하는 구성원처럼 행위하라 |

## Critique of Pure Reason | 순수이성비판

**English:**

### The Central Question:
> "What can we know?"

### Key Distinctions:

| Type | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **A priori** | Knowledge independent of experience |
| **A posteriori** | Knowledge gained from experience |
| **Analytic** | Truth by definition (predicate in subject) |
| **Synthetic** | Truth adds new information |

### Kant's Revolution:
> "The mind plays an active role in constituting the features of experience and limiting the mind's access only to the empirical realm of space and time."

### Limits of Knowledge:
> "It is impossible to extend knowledge to the supersensible realm of speculative metaphysics."

We can only know phenomena (things as they appear), not noumena (things in themselves).

**한국어:**

### 중심 질문:
> "우리는 무엇을 알 수 있는가?"

### 주요 구분:

| 유형 | 의미 |
|-----|-----|
| **선험적** | 경험과 무관한 지식 |
| **후험적** | 경험에서 얻은 지식 |
| **분석적** | 정의에 의한 진리 |
| **종합적** | 새로운 정보를 추가하는 진리 |

## Deontological Ethics | 의무론적 윤리학

**English:**

> "Kant is the primary proponent in history of deontological ethics. On Kant's view, the sole feature that gives an action moral worth is not the outcome that is achieved by the action, but the motive that is behind the action."

### Key Principles:

1. **Duty**: Morality is about doing one's duty, not seeking happiness
2. **Good Will**: Only a good will is good without qualification
3. **Autonomy**: We give the moral law to ourselves
4. **Rationality**: Morality is grounded in reason, not emotion

### The Good Will:
> "Nothing in the world—indeed nothing even beyond the world—can possibly be conceived which could be called good without qualification except a good will."

**한국어:**

> "칸트는 역사상 의무론적 윤리학의 주요 옹호자입니다. 칸트의 관점에서 행위에 도덕적 가치를 부여하는 유일한 특성은 행위에 의해 달성되는 결과가 아니라 행위 뒤에 있는 동기입니다."

### 핵심 원리:

1. **의무**: 도덕은 행복 추구가 아닌 의무 수행에 관한 것
2. **선의지**: 오직 선의지만이 무조건적으로 선함
3. **자율**: 우리가 도덕법을 스스로에게 부여
4. **합리성**: 도덕은 감정이 아닌 이성에 근거
