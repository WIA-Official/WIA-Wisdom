# Simone de Beauvoir | 시몬 드 보부아르

> The Ethics of Ambiguity: Freedom, Responsibility, and Becoming

---

## Overview | 개요

Simone de Beauvoir (1908–1986) was a French existentialist philosopher, writer, social theorist, and feminist activist. Her most influential work, *The Second Sex* (1949), is considered a foundational text of contemporary feminism. Her philosophical work, particularly *The Ethics of Ambiguity* (1947), developed existentialist ethics emphasizing freedom, responsibility, and the inherent ambiguity of human existence.

> "One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman." This famous statement encapsulates Beauvoir's central insight that gender is not a biological given but a social construction—a becoming rather than a being.

She was a lifelong companion of Jean-Paul Sartre, though her philosophical contributions were distinct and independently significant.

> "여자는 태어나는 것이 아니라 만들어지는 것이다." 이 유명한 문장은 젠더가 생물학적 주어짐이 아니라 사회적 구성—존재가 아니라 되어감—이라는 보부아르의 핵심 통찰을 요약합니다.
---
## The Ethics of Ambiguity | 애매성의 윤리학
> Beauvoir argues that human existence is fundamentally ambiguous—we are both subjects (consciousness, freedom) and objects (body, facticity). This ambiguity cannot be resolved but must be lived.

> "To exist genuinely is not to deny this ambiguity but to assume it."

Key concepts:
- **Ambiguity**: The irreducible tension between freedom and facticity
- **Authentic existence**: Embracing ambiguity rather than fleeing it
- **Freedom**: Both gift and burden, always situated
- **Responsibility**: We are responsible for what we make of ourselves

> 보부아르는 인간 존재가 근본적으로 애매하다고 주장합니다—우리는 주체 (의식, 자유)이자 객체 (신체, 사실성)입니다. 이 애매성은 해결될 수 없고 살아져야 합니다.
> "진정으로 존재한다는 것은 이 애매성을 부정하는 것이 아니라 그것을 떠맡는 것이다."
---
## The Second Sex | 제2의 성
> "One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman." This statement distinguishes between sex (biological) and gender (social construction), arguing that femininity is not natural but learned.

> Woman has been defined as "the Other"—not the subject but the object, not the essential but the inessential. Throughout history, man has been the norm, the standard, while woman has been the deviation.

Central insights:
- Woman as "the Other"
- Gender as social construction
- Oppression through immanence
- Liberation through transcendence

> "The body is not a thing, it is a situation... it is the instrument of our grasp upon the world."

> "여자는 태어나는 것이 아니라 만들어지는 것이다." 이 진술은 성 (생물학적)과 젠더 (사회적 구성)를 구별하며, 여성성이 자연적인 것이 아니라 학습된다고 주장합니다.
> 여성은 "타자"로 정의되어 왔습니다—주체가 아니라 객체, 본질적인 것이 아니라 비본질적인 것. 역사 전체에서 남성이 규범, 기준이었고, 여성은 일탈이었습니다.

## Freedom and Oppression | 자유와 억압
> Beauvoir extends Sartrean existentialism by examining how oppression limits freedom. While we are "condemned to be free," oppressive structures (patriarchy, colonialism, class) constrain the exercise of that freedom.

> True freedom requires the freedom of others. "I am oppressed if I am thrown into a category... The liberation of women will be the liberation of all."

> We cannot be free alone. My freedom depends on yours, and true liberation requires solidarity with all oppressed peoples.

> 보부아르는 억압이 어떻게 자유를 제한하는지 검토함으로써 사르트르적 실존주의를 확장합니다. 우리가 "자유롭도록 운명지어져" 있지만, 억압적 구조 (가부장제, 식민주의, 계급)가 그 자유의 행사를 제약합니다.
> 진정한 자유는 타인의 자유를 필요로 합니다. "나는 범주에 던져지면 억압받는다... 여성의 해방은 모든 이의 해방이 될 것이다."
---
## Legacy and Influence | 유산과 영향
> Beauvoir's work has profoundly influenced feminist philosophy, gender studies, and existentialist ethics. *The Second Sex* is considered the foundational text of second-wave feminism.

Her influence extends to:
- Feminist philosophy and theory
- Gender and queer studies
- Existentialist ethics
- Social and political philosophy

> "I wish that every human life might be pure transparent freedom."

> 보부아르의 작품은 페미니스트 철학, 젠더 연구, 실존주의 윤리학에 심대한 영향을 미쳤습니다. 『제2의 성』은 제2물결 페미니즘의 기초 텍스트로 여겨집니다.
