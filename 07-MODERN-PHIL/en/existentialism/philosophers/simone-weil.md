# Simone Weil | 시몬 베유

> The Mystic Philosopher of Attention: Gravity, Grace, and Affliction

---

## Overview | 개요

Simone Weil (1909–1943) was a French philosopher, mystic, and political activist—one of the most original and demanding thinkers of the twentieth century. She defies easy categorization: ascetic and mystic, teacher and factory worker, labor activist and political militant, social thinker and moral psychologist, critical Marxist and heterodox Christian theologian.

Her central philosophical concepts—attention (*l'attention*), gravity and grace (*pesanteur et grâce*), and affliction (*malheur*)—form a profound phenomenology of spiritual life. Writing from deep personal experience of suffering and solidarity with the oppressed, Weil developed a philosophy that insists on the reality of both human misery and transcendent grace.

Her works, most published posthumously from her notebooks by friends like Gustave Thibon, include *Gravity and Grace*, *Waiting for God*, and *The Need for Roots*.

## Attention (L'Attention) | 주의
### The Central Concept | 중심 개념
Weil's most important ethical and spiritual concept is *attention*—a particular quality of receptive openness:

> Attention consists of suspending or emptying one's thought, such that one is ready to receive—to be penetrated by—the object to which one turns one's gaze, be that object one's neighbor, or ultimately, God.

Attention is not concentration or effort but rather a kind of waiting, a willingness to be affected by reality without imposing one's own agenda or preconceptions.

Key aspects:
- **Emptying the ego**: Renouncing self-centered interpretation
- **Receptivity**: Being open to what is
- **Consent**: Allowing reality to enter
- **Love**: Attention is the substance of love

> "The capacity to give one's attention to a sufferer is a very rare and difficult thing: it is almost a miracle; it is a miracle."

> 주의는 자신의 생각을 중단하거나 비우는 것으로, 시선을 돌리는 대상—그것이 이웃이든, 궁극적으로 하나님이든—에 의해 받아들이고 관통되도록 준비되는 것입니다.
### Attention and Education | 주의와 교육
Weil believed that the true purpose of education is to develop the faculty of attention. Even studying geometry or Latin—subjects that may never be "used"—trains the soul in attention, which can then be directed toward God and neighbor.

## Gravity and Grace | 중력과 은총
### The Two Forces | 두 가지 힘
Weil's metaphysics distinguishes between two fundamental forces operating in the world:

**Gravity (Pesanteur):**
- The natural downward pull on all created beings
- Physical, psychological, and social necessity
- The force that drags attention away from God
- Includes ego, self-interest, and mechanical reaction

**Grace (Grâce):**
- The supernatural counterbalance to gravity
- The motivation by and goodness of God
- Pierces the world of necessity
- Orients, harmonizes, and balances

> Grace, entering the empirical world, disposes one to be purified by leaving the void open, waiting for a good that is real but that could never "exist" in a material sense.

## Affliction (Malheur) | 고난
### Beyond Ordinary Suffering | 일반적 고통을 넘어
*Malheur* (affliction) is Weil's term for a specific kind of suffering that exceeds ordinary pain:

> Affliction is a condition where the human being feels cut off not only from society but from God, justice, and hope. Affliction resembles what we might today describe as profound existential trauma.

Affliction has three dimensions:
1. **Physical**: Bodily pain and degradation
2. **Psychological**: Humiliation, abandonment
3. **Social**: Being reduced to an object, loss of dignity

The afflicted person becomes invisible, marked, reduced to a thing. Yet Weil saw affliction as potentially redemptive—it strips away illusion and opens the possibility of genuine spiritual transformation.

> "In the realm of suffering, affliction is something apart, specific, and irreducible. It is quite a different thing from simple suffering. It takes possession of the soul and marks it through and through with its own particular mark."

> 고난은 인간 존재가 사회뿐만 아니라 하나님, 정의, 희망에서도 단절되었다고 느끼는 상태입니다. 고난은 우리가 오늘날 심오한 실존적 트라우마로 묘사할 것과 닮았습니다.

## Decreation | 탈창조
Weil developed the paradoxical concept of *décréation*—a kind of un-creating of the self:

- We exist by God's creative act
- True love of God requires surrendering that existence back
- Not suicide but spiritual emptying
- Making room for God by removing self

This is not nihilism but the ultimate generosity—returning the gift of existence to the Giver.

---
## Life and Witness | 삶과 증언
Weil lived her philosophy with radical consistency:

- Worked in Renault factory to understand worker oppression
- Joined Spanish Civil War as anarchist militia
- Reduced her food intake to solidarity with occupied France
- Died at 34, partly from tuberculosis, partly from self-starvation

Her life embodied her teaching: that attention to suffering and solidarity with the afflicted is the path to encountering the sacred.
