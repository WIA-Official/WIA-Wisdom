# Authenticity | 진정성

> Being True to Oneself: The Existentialist Ideal

---

## Overview | 개요

Authenticity is a central concept in existentialist philosophy, referring to the condition of living in accordance with one's true self rather than conforming to external pressures, social expectations, or self-deception. The word we translate as 'authenticity' derives from Heidegger's neologism *Eigentlichkeit*, built on the stem *eigen*, meaning 'own' or 'proper', implying "owning up to and owning what one is and does."

For existentialists like Heidegger, Sartre, and Beauvoir, authenticity represents the fundamental challenge of human existence: to take responsibility for creating meaning and becoming who we truly are.

## Heidegger's Conception | 하이데거의 개념

> "Heidegger's idea of authenticity can be traced from his conception of Being-onto-death. Humans' awareness of their finitude causes existential anxiety, leading them to escape into what Heidegger calls *Das Man* ('the They')—the impersonal domain of social convention."

### Key Elements:

| Concept | German | Meaning |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Authenticity** | Eigentlichkeit | Owning one's existence |
| **Inauthenticity** | Uneigentlichkeit | Lost in "the They" |
| **The They** | Das Man | Impersonal social convention |
| **Being-toward-death** | Sein-zum-Tode | Awareness of finitude |

### Freedom Toward Death:
> "For Heidegger, authenticity requires 'readiness' or 'anticipation' (*Vorgriff*), passionately holding oneself open for life's breakdowns—'an impassioned freedom towards death—a freedom which has been released from the illusions of the They.'"

> "하이데거의 진정성 개념은 죽음을 향한 존재 개념에서 추적될 수 있습니다. 유한성에 대한 인간의 인식은 실존적 불안을 야기하여 하이데거가 *Das Man*('그들')이라고 부르는 것으로 도피하게 합니다."

## Sartre and Bad Faith | 사르트르와 자기기만

> "Sartre defines an authentic human being as one who chooses 'in a way which reflects the nature of the for-itself as both transcendence and facticity.'"

### Bad Faith (Mauvaise Foi):
> "By clinging to identity, we live in what Sartre calls 'bad faith'—deceiving ourselves about the limits of our own freedom, convincing ourselves we have to be a certain way when more possibilities are available."

> "A lack of proper coordination between transcendence and facticity constitutes bad faith, either at an individual or an inter-personal level."

### The Famous Waiter Example:
Sartre describes a waiter who is too perfectly playing his role—his movements are mechanical, exaggerated. He is playing at being a waiter rather than authentically being one. This illustrates how we can hide from our freedom by over-identifying with social roles.

### 자기기만 (Mauvaise Foi):
> "정체성에 집착함으로써 우리는 사르트르가 '자기기만'이라고 부르는 것 속에 삽니다—자신의 자유의 한계에 대해 스스로를 속이며, 더 많은 가능성이 있을 때 특정한 방식이어야 한다고 자신을 설득합니다."

## Heidegger vs. Sartre | 하이데거 vs. 사르트르

> "Both Sartre and Heidegger agree that we act in context, and the delimitation of authenticity rests in the freedom to choose an action in commitment with the self, in contrast to unreflexive action in conformity with available social constructs."

### Differences:

| Aspect | Heidegger | Sartre |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Focus** | Being-toward-death | Radical freedom |
| **Context** | Historical/temporal | Individual choice |
| **Approach** | Ontological | Phenomenological |

### Sartre's Critique:
> "Sartre himself attacked Heidegger for introducing authenticity as a way of providing something foundational in an otherwise contingent world."

> "사르트르와 하이데거 모두 우리가 맥락에서 행동한다는 데 동의하며, 진정성의 경계는 이용 가능한 사회적 구성에 따른 비성찰적 행동과 대조되는 자아와의 헌신 속에서 행동을 선택하는 자유에 있습니다."
| 측면 | 하이데거 | 사르트르 |
|-----|---------|---------|
| **초점** | 죽음을 향한 존재 | 급진적 자유 |
| **맥락** | 역사적/시간적 | 개인적 선택 |
| **접근** | 존재론적 | 현상학적 |

## Critiques | 비판

> "Sartre, Simone de Beauvoir, and Marjorie Grene criticized Heidegger's emphasis on death in creating authenticity as too individualistic and abstract. Beauvoir insisted that authenticity must be thought of in terms of a specific project the individual undertakes to give one's life meaning."

### Beauvoir's View:
Authenticity is not abstract but concrete—it requires:
- Engagement with specific projects
- Recognition of situation
- Solidarity with others
- Commitment to freedom

> "사르트르, 시몬 드 보부아르, 마조리 그린은 진정성 창조에서 죽음에 대한 하이데거의 강조가 너무 개인주의적이고 추상적이라고 비판했습니다. 보부아르는 진정성이 개인이 삶에 의미를 부여하기 위해 수행하는 구체적 프로젝트의 관점에서 생각되어야 한다고 주장했습니다."
