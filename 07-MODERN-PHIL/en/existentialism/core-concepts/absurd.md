# The Absurd | 부조리 (L'Absurde)

> The Confrontation Between Human Meaning and Cosmic Silence

---

## Overview | 개요

The Absurd is a central concept in 20th-century philosophy, particularly in the works of Albert Camus. It refers to the fundamental tension that arises from the confrontation between humanity's innate search for meaning, order, and purpose, and the universe's complete indifference and silence in response. The Absurd is not the world itself, nor the human mind, but the clash between them.

This concept has roots in Søren Kierkegaard's existentialist philosophy and was fully developed by Camus in works like *The Myth of Sisyphus*. The question of how to respond to the Absurd—suicide, religious faith, or rebellion—became one of the defining philosophical issues of the modern era.

## The Absurd Condition | 부조리한 상황

> "The Absurd is created because man, who is placed in an unintelligent universe, realizes that human values are not founded on a solid external component."

### Components of the Absurd:
| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Human Need** | Desire for meaning, order, rationality |
| **Cosmic Silence** | Universe offers no response |
| **The Gap** | Unbridgeable distance between the two |
| **Consciousness** | Awareness of this condition |

> "Importantly for Camus, this doesn't mean that life has no meaning, only that its significance is always beyond us."

## Three Responses | 세 가지 반응

> "The three responses discussed in the traditional absurdist literature are suicide, religious belief in a higher purpose, and rebellion against the absurd."

### 1. Physical Suicide (Rejected by Camus):
> "Suicide is an option that Camus firmly dismisses as the renunciation of human values and freedom."

- Treats the Absurd as conclusive
- Abandons the struggle
- Surrenders consciousness

### 2. Philosophical Suicide / Leap of Faith (Rejected):
- Embracing religion or transcendent meaning
- Kierkegaard's "leap of faith"
- Camus calls this escaping the Absurd
- "They deify what crushes them"

### 3. Rebellion (Camus's Recommendation):
> "The answer is to live without escape and with integrity, in 'revolt' and defiance."

- Acknowledge the Absurd
- Refuse to escape
- Create meaning despite silence
- Live with lucidity

## Kierkegaard vs. Camus | 키르케고르 대 카뮈

| Aspect | Kierkegaard | Camus |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Response** | Leap of faith | Revolt without escape |
| **Religion** | Embrace the absurd through faith | Reject as "philosophical suicide" |
| **Freedom** | Found in religious commitment | Found in defiant living |
| **Hope** | In transcendence | In the struggle itself |

> "Camus states that because the leap of faith escapes rationality and defers to abstraction over personal experience, the leap of faith is not absurd."

| 측면 | 키르케고르 | 카뮈 |

## Sisyphus: The Absurd Hero | 시지프: 부조리의 영웅

> "Camus has left us with a striking image of the human fate: Sisyphus endlessly pushing his rock up the mountain only to see it roll back down each time."

### Why Sisyphus Is Happy:
- He fully owns his fate
- His struggle creates meaning
- The revolt itself is the purpose
- Consciousness transforms punishment into triumph

> "One must imagine Sisyphus happy."
