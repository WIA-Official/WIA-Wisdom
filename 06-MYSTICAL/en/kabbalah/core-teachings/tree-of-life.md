# The Tree of Life | 생명의 나무 (עֵץ חַיִּים)

> Etz Chaim: The Map of Divine Emanation

---

## Overview | 개요

The Tree of Life (Hebrew: עֵץ חַיִּים, Etz Chaim) is the primary mystical symbol of Kabbalah, the Jewish mystical tradition. It is a diagram depicting the ten Sefirot (divine emanations) through which Ein Sof (the Infinite) reveals itself and creates the universe. The Tree represents both the structure of creation and a map of the human soul, providing a path for spiritual ascent back to the Divine.

The Tree of Life is not merely theoretical but serves as a practical framework for meditation, prayer, and spiritual development. Each of its ten spheres represents an aspect of God's interaction with creation and a corresponding quality within human consciousness.

---
## Ein Sof: The Infinite | 아인 소프: 무한
Before the Sefirot is Ein Sof (lit. "without end")—the Infinite aspect of God that transcends all comprehension:

> "Ein Sof represents the formless state of the universe before the self-materialization of God. It is the hidden God about whom nothing can be known."

From Ein Sof emanates Ohr Ein Sof (Endless Light), which flows through the Sefirot to create and sustain all worlds. This light is progressively "filtered" through each sphere, becoming more accessible as it descends.

> "아인 소프는 하나님의 자기 물질화 이전 우주의 형태 없는 상태를 나타냅니다. 이것은 아무것도 알 수 없는 숨겨진 하나님입니다."

## The Ten Sefirot | 열 개의 세피로트
The Sefirot (singular: Sefirah) are the ten attributes through which God manifests:

### The Three Pillars

| Pillar | Quality | Sefirot |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Right | Mercy (Chesed) | Chokmah, Chesed, Netzach |
| Left | Judgment (Din) | Binah, Gevurah, Hod |
| Middle | Balance | Keter, Tiferet, Yesod, Malkhut |

### The Ten Sefirot in Detail:

| # | Sefirah | Meaning | Quality |
|---|---------|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Keter** | Crown | Divine Will, unity with Ein Sof |
| 2 | **Chokmah** | Wisdom | Primordial masculine energy, first flash of creation |
| 3 | **Binah** | Understanding | Feminine receptivity, the womb of creation |
| 4 | **Chesed** | Loving-kindness | Unlimited giving, grace, mercy |
| 5 | **Gevurah** | Strength/Judgment | Discipline, boundaries, awe |
| 6 | **Tiferet** | Beauty/Harmony | Balance of mercy and judgment |
| 7 | **Netzach** | Victory/Eternity | Perseverance, ambition |
| 8 | **Hod** | Splendor/Glory | Humility, surrender |
| 9 | **Yesod** | Foundation | Connection, covenant, transmission |
| 10 | **Malkhut** | Kingdom | Manifestation in the physical world, Shekhinah |

## The Flow of Divine Energy | 신성한 에너지의 흐름
Kabbalists describe the Sefirot as a system through which divine energy flows from the infinite to the finite:

> "The Kabbalists often referred to the structure of the Sefirot as a system of pipes through which the divine emanation—the concealed Ein Sof—flows down to humans on earth."

### The Lightning Flash
The path of creation follows the "lightning flash" (סדר השתלשלות), descending from Keter through each Sefirah to Malkhut—from pure divine consciousness to physical reality.

### Ascending the Tree
Spiritual practice involves ascending the Tree—raising consciousness from Malkhut (ordinary awareness) back through the Sefirot toward union with the Divine.

> "카발리스트들은 세피로트의 구조를 숨겨진 아인 소프—신성한 유출—가 땅의 인간에게로 흘러내리는 관들의 체계로 자주 언급했습니다."
---
## Da'at: The Hidden Sefirah | 다아트: 숨겨진 세피라
An eleventh sefirah, Da'at (Knowledge), sometimes appears on the Tree:

- Located between Chokmah and Binah
- Represents the union of Wisdom and Understanding
- Not always counted because it is not a separate emanation
- Considered the "knowledge" that emerges from wisdom meeting understanding

Da'at is hidden because true knowledge transcends subject-object duality.

## The Four Worlds | 네 세계
The Tree of Life exists in each of the Four Worlds:

| World | Hebrew | Realm |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **Atzilut** | אצילות | Emanation, closest to Ein Sof |
| **Beriah** | בריאה | Creation, realm of the Throne |
| **Yetzirah** | יצירה | Formation, realm of angels |
| **Assiyah** | עשייה | Action, physical world |

Each world contains its own Tree, and spiritual ascent moves through all four.

| 세계 | 히브리어 | 영역 |
|-----|---------|-----|
| **아칠룻** | אצילות | 유출, 아인 소프에 가장 가까움 |
| **베리아** | בריאה | 창조, 보좌의 영역 |
| **예치라** | יצירה | 형성, 천사의 영역 |
| **아시야** | עשייה | 행동, 물질 세계 |

## Practical Application | 실용적 적용
The Tree of Life provides a framework for:

### Meditation
- Contemplating individual Sefirot
- Visualizing the flow of light
- Ascending through the spheres

### Character Development
- Each Sefirah corresponds to a character trait to develop
- Balancing Chesed (mercy) with Gevurah (discipline)
- Cultivating Tiferet (balance) in daily life

### Understanding Self and World
- Mapping psychological states onto the Tree
- Understanding how divine qualities manifest
- Seeing correspondences between macrocosm and microcosm
