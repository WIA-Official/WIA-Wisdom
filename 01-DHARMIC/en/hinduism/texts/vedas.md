# The Vedas | 베다 (वेद)

> The Oldest Sacred Texts of Humanity

---

## Overview | 개요

The Vedas (Sanskrit: वेद, "knowledge") are a large body of religious texts originating in ancient India, constituting the oldest scriptures of Hinduism and the oldest layer of Sanskrit literature. Composed in Vedic Sanskrit over roughly a millennium (c. 1500-500 BCE), they are considered foundational to Hindu philosophy, culture, and spirituality.

The Vedas are regarded as apauruṣeya (not of human origin) in Hindu tradition—revealed to ancient sages (Rishis) through deep meditation. They are also called Śruti ("that which is heard"), emphasizing their divine revelation.

## The Four Vedas | 네 베다

> "There are four Vedas: the Rigveda, the Yajurveda, the Samaveda and the Atharvaveda."

| Veda | Focus | Content |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Rigveda** | Praise | 1,028 hymns to deities |
| **Samaveda** | Melody | Chants for rituals |
| **Yajurveda** | Ritual | Prose mantras for ceremonies |
| **Atharvaveda** | Magic | Spells, healing, philosophy |

### Rigveda (ऋग्वेद):
> "The Rigveda is the oldest known Vedic Sanskrit text. Its early layers are among the oldest extant texts in any Indo-European language."

### Samaveda (सामवेद):
> "The verses of the Samaveda are taken almost completely from the Rigveda, but arranged differently so they may be chanted."

### Yajurveda (यजुर्वेद):
> "The Yajurveda is divided into the White and Black halves and contains prose commentaries on how rituals and sacrifices should be performed."

### Atharvaveda (अथर्ववेद):
> "The Atharvaveda includes charms and magic incantations written in the style of folklore."

## Structure of Each Veda | 각 베다의 구조

> "Each Veda has four subdivisions:"

| Division | Sanskrit | Content |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Samhitas** | संहिता | Mantras and hymns |
| **Brahmanas** | ब्राह्मण | Ritual commentaries |
| **Aranyakas** | आरण्यक | Forest texts; symbolic interpretations |
| **Upanishads** | उपनिषद् | Philosophical teachings |

### Progression:
- Samhitas: Outer rituals
- Brahmanas: Meaning of rituals
- Aranyakas: Inner symbolism
- Upanishads: Philosophical essence

## Divine Origin | 신성한 기원

> "The four Vedas are considered apauruṣeya, meaning they do not have a human author but were revealed to ancient sages through deep meditative states, by a divine entity or supreme being (Param Brahman)."

### Oral Preservation:
> "Most scholars believe that the sounds and texts of the Rigveda have been orally transmitted with precision since the 2nd millennium BCE, through methods of memorisation of exceptional complexity, rigour and fidelity."

> "Memorisation of the sacred Vedas included up to eleven forms of recitation of the same text."

### UNESCO Recognition:
> "Rigveda manuscripts were selected for inscription in UNESCO's Memory of the World Register in 2007."

> "네 베다는 아파우루셰야로 여겨지며, 인간 저자가 없고 깊은 명상 상태에서 신성한 존재 또는 최고 존재(파람 브라만)에 의해 고대 성인들에게 계시되었음을 의미합니다."
### 구전 보존:
> "대부분의 학자들은 리그베다의 소리와 텍스트가 기원전 2천년기 이래 예외적인 복잡성, 엄격함, 충실성의 암기 방법을 통해 정확하게 구전되어 왔다고 믿습니다."

## Key Teachings | 핵심 가르침

### Cosmic Order (Rta):
The Vedas speak of Rta (ऋत)—the cosmic order underlying all existence.

### The Devas:
Major deities include:
- Agni (fire)
- Indra (storm/war)
- Varuna (cosmic order)
- Surya (sun)
- Ushas (dawn)

### Famous Hymns:
- Nasadiya Sukta (Creation hymn) - "There was neither existence nor non-existence..."
- Purusha Sukta - The cosmic person
- Gayatri Mantra - The most sacred verse
