# The Upanishads | 우파니샤드

> The Culmination of Vedic Wisdom: "That Thou Art"

---

## Overview | 개요

The Upanishads (Sanskrit: उपनिषद्, "sitting near") are the final and most philosophical portion of the Vedas, composed between c. 800-200 BCE. Often called Vedanta ("end of the Vedas"), they mark the culmination of Vedic thought, shifting from ritual sacrifice to profound inquiry into the nature of reality, the self, and liberation.

Unlike earlier Vedic texts focused on rituals, the Upanishads explore fundamental questions: What is the ultimate reality? What is the true self? How does one achieve liberation? Their answers—centering on Brahman (ultimate reality) and Atman (the true self)—became the foundation of Hindu philosophy and the Vedanta schools.

---
## Brahman: The Ultimate Reality | 브라만: 궁극적 현실
Brahman (ब्रह्मन्) is the central concept of the Upanishads—the unchanging, infinite, ultimate reality underlying all existence:

> "Brahman is described as the unchanging 'primordial reality' that creates, sustains, and ultimately withdraws the universe within itself."

### Characteristics of Brahman:
- **Sat** (Being): Pure existence
- **Chit** (Consciousness): Pure awareness
- **Ananda** (Bliss): Pure joy
- Beyond all qualities (nirguna) yet source of all qualities (saguna)
- Immanent in all things yet transcending them

Brahman is not a personal god but the ground of all being—that from which everything arises, by which everything is sustained, and into which everything returns.

## Atman: The True Self | 아트만: 진정한 자아
Atman (आत्मन्) is the innermost self, the eternal essence within each being:

> "The Vedic sages recognized a higher self within the self—the Atman—which was a part of Brahman each individual carried within."

### Key Points:
- Not the ego or personality
- Not the body or mind
- The unchanging witness of all experience
- Identical with Brahman in its deepest nature
- "The Atman could grasp Brahman because the Atman was Brahman"

Realizing the identity of Atman and Brahman is the goal of spiritual life.

> "베다 현자들은 자아 안에 더 높은 자아—아트만—을 인식했으며, 이것은 각 개인이 내면에 지닌 브라만의 일부였습니다."

## The Mahāvākyas | 마하바키야 (위대한 말씀)
Shankara identified four Great Sayings (Mahāvākyas) that express the core teaching:

| Mahāvākya | Upanishad | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Prajñānam Brahma** | Aitareya | "Consciousness is Brahman" |
| **Aham Brahmāsmi** | Brihadaranyaka | "I am Brahman" |
| **Tat Tvam Asi** | Chandogya | "That Thou Art" |
| **Ayam Ātmā Brahma** | Mandukya | "This Atman is Brahman" |

These express the non-dual relationship between the individual self and ultimate reality.

## Liberation (Moksha) | 해탈 (목샤)
The Upanishads teach that liberation comes through knowledge (jñāna):

> "The experiential knowledge of the relationship between the human soul (atman) and the supreme being (brahman) is said to bring an end to the cycle of birth, death, and rebirth."

### The Path:
1. **Śravaṇa**: Hearing the teachings
2. **Manana**: Reflecting on them deeply
3. **Nididhyāsana**: Meditating until they become direct experience

When one truly realizes "I am Brahman," ignorance dissolves and liberation is attained.

> "인간 영혼(아트만)과 최고 존재(브라만) 사이의 관계에 대한 경험적 지식이 탄생, 죽음, 재탄생의 순환을 끝낸다고 합니다."
---
## Maya: The World Illusion | 마야: 세계 환상
> "The Upanishads describe the phenomenal world (Maya) as impermanent and illusory, emphasizing that true self-realization leads to liberation from the cycle of birth and death (Samsara)."

Maya is not that the world doesn't exist, but that it is not what it appears to be. We mistake the changing for the unchanging, the finite for the infinite.

> "우파니샤드는 현상 세계(마야)를 무상하고 환상적인 것으로 묘사하며, 진정한 자기 실현이 탄생과 죽음의 순환(삼사라)에서 해탈로 이끈다고 강조합니다."

## Vedanta Schools | 베단타 학파
The Upanishads gave rise to multiple interpretations:

| School | Teacher | View |
|--------|---------|------|
| **Advaita** | Shankara | Non-dualism: Atman is Brahman |
| **Vishishtadvaita** | Ramanuja | Qualified non-dualism |
| **Dvaita** | Madhva | Dualism: Atman and Brahman are distinct |

All agree on the authority of the Upanishads but differ in interpretation.

| 학파 | 스승 | 견해 |
|-----|-----|-----|
| **아드바이타** | 샹카라 | 비이원론: 아트만이 브라만 |
| **비시슈타드바이타** | 라마누자 | 한정 비이원론 |
| **드바이타** | 마드바 | 이원론: 아트만과 브라만은 구별됨 |
