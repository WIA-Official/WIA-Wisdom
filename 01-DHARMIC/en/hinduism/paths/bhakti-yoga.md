# Bhakti Yoga | 박티 요가 (信愛의 道)

> The Path of Devotion: Loving God as the Supreme Practice

---

## Overview | 개요

Bhakti Yoga is the Hindu spiritual path of devotion and loving surrender to the Divine. Among the four classical yogas—*jnana* (knowledge), *karma* (action), *raja* (meditation), and *bhakti* (devotion)—bhakti is often considered the most accessible and emotionally satisfying path, open to all regardless of caste, education, or spiritual attainment.

The *Narada Bhakti Sutra*, a classical text attributed to the sage Narada, defines bhakti as "the most elevated, pure love for God" which is eternal by nature and through which one obtains perfect peace and liberation from *samsara* (the cycle of rebirth). According to Narada, bhakti is the easiest way to attain the Divine because it is our very nature to love and be loved.

This path emphasizes personal relationship with God, expressed through worship, chanting, service, and the cultivation of divine love that transforms the devotee's entire being.

## The Narada Bhakti Sutra | 나라다 박티 수트라
### Nature of Bhakti | 박티의 본성
> The Narada Bhakti Sutra is a well-known sutra venerated within the traditions of Hinduism, reportedly spoken by the famous sage Narada. The text details the process of devotion (Bhakti), or Bhakti yoga.

> Sri Ramakrishna, the mystic-saint, hailed this work as "The best path to union with God is to follow the way of divine love as taught by Narada."

The 84 aphorisms (sutras) are structured:
1. **Sutras 1-24**: The nature of bhakti
2. **Sutras 25-33**: Superiority of bhakti to other paths
3. **Sutras 34-50**: Methods of practice
4. **Sutras 51-66**: Signs of a true devotee
5. **Sutras 67-84**: Glory of realized souls

> 나라다 박티 수트라는 힌두교 전통 내에서 숭배되는 잘 알려진 수트라로, 유명한 현자 나라다가 말했다고 전해집니다. 텍스트는 헌신 (박티), 또는 박티 요가의 과정을 자세히 설명합니다.
> 신비 성인 스리 라마크리슈나는 이 작품을 "하나님과의 합일에 가장 좋은 길은 나라다가 가르친 신적 사랑의 길을 따르는 것"이라고 칭송했습니다.
### Definition | 정의
> Bhakti itself is defined as being "the most elevated, pure love for God" which is eternal by nature and through following which one obtains perfect peace and immortality (release from samsara). The symptoms of such devotion are that one no longer has any selfish desires, nor is affected by the dualities of loss or gain.

> According to Narada, bhakti is the easiest way to attain the Divine because it is our very nature to love and be loved.

> 박티 자체는 "가장 높고 순수한 하나님에 대한 사랑"으로 정의되며, 이것은 본성상 영원하고 이를 따름으로써 완전한 평화와 불멸 (삼사라에서의 해방)을 얻습니다. 그러한 헌신의 증상은 더 이상 이기적 욕망이 없고, 상실이나 이득의 이원성에 영향받지 않는 것입니다.

## Two Forms of Bhakti | 두 가지 형태의 박티
The *Narada Bhakti Sutra* distinguishes two levels of devotion:

### Apara Bhakti (Lower Devotion) | 아파라 박티 (하위 헌신)

Ways to cultivate and deepen devotion:
- Rituals and worship
- Chanting and singing
- Pilgrimage
- Service to devotees
- Study of scriptures

### Para Bhakti (Higher Devotion) | 파라 박티 (상위 헌신)

> Para Bhakti or Higher devotion is the goal. It is Para Bhakti that leads to Mukti (liberation).

Characteristics:
- Complete absorption in God
- Spontaneous, unconditional love
- No distinction between devotee and Divine
- Continuous awareness of God's presence
- Perfect peace and bliss

### 아파라 박티 (하위 헌신)
### 파라 박티 (상위 헌신)
> 파라 박티 또는 상위 헌신이 목표입니다. 목티 (해방)로 이끄는 것은 파라 박티입니다.

## Nine Forms of Bhakti | 아홉 가지 형태의 박티
The *Bhagavata Purana* enumerates nine modes of devotional practice:

| Form | Sanskrit | Practice |
|------|----------|----------|
| 1. Listening | *Shravana* | Hearing about God's glories |
| 2. Singing | *Kirtana* | Chanting God's names |
| 3. Remembrance | *Smarana* | Constant remembrance |
| 4. Service | *Pada-sevana* | Serving God's feet/devotees |
| 5. Worship | *Archana* | Ritual worship |
| 6. Prostration | *Vandana* | Bowing in humility |
| 7. Servitude | *Dasya* | Serving as God's servant |
| 8. Friendship | *Sakhya* | Relating as friend |
| 9. Self-offering | *Atma-nivedana* | Complete surrender |

## Key Principles | 핵심 원리
> The Narada Bhakti Sutra outlines several key principles: Devotion (Bhakti)—cultivating a loving relationship with God; Surrender (Saranagati)—acknowledging one's limitations and surrendering to the divine will; Faith (Shraddha)—trusting in God's grace; and Selflessness (Nishkama)—practicing devotion without any desire for personal gain.

The Sage Narada:
> Of all the Vedic ṛṣis ("seers"), Narada is the inventor of music, the inspirer of poets, and the healer for the broken-hearted. Nārada means a "gift" (*da*) to "humanity" (*nāra*).

> 나라다 박티 수트라는 여러 핵심 원리를 개요합니다: 헌신 (박티)—하나님과 사랑스러운 관계 함양; 항복 (사라나가티)—자신의 한계를 인정하고 신적 의지에 항복; 믿음 (슈라다)—하나님의 은총을 신뢰; 무사심 (니슈카마)—개인적 이득에 대한 욕망 없이 헌신 실천.

## The Bhakti Movement | 박티 운동
The bhakti movement swept across India from the 7th century onwards:

- **South India**: Alvars and Nayanars (Tamil devotional poets)
- **North India**: Kabir, Mirabai, Tulsidas, Surdas
- **Bengal**: Chaitanya Mahaprabhu, ISKCON
- **Maharashtra**: Sant Tukaram, Dnyaneshwar

These saints taught that sincere devotion transcends caste, gender, and formal learning.
