# Aparigraha | 아파리그라하 (無執著)

> Non-Possessiveness: Freedom Through Detachment

---

## Overview | 개요

In Jainism, aparigraha is the virtue of non-possessiveness, non-grasping, or non-greediness. It is one of the five main ethical vows (Panch Maha Vratas) that every Jain follower observes. The core of Jain philosophy is established on three ethical pillars: ahiṃsā (nonviolence), anekāntavāda (non-absolutism), and aparigraha (non-possession).

Aparigraha teaches that true happiness does not stem from accumulating wealth or possessions but rather from cultivating a sense of inner peace and contentment with what one has.

## Two Types of Attachment | 두 가지 집착

> "Jainism views attachments to material or emotional possessions as what leads to passions, which in turn leads to violence."

> "Jain texts say that 'attachment to possessions' (parigraha) is of two kinds: attachment to internal possessions (ābhyantara parigraha), and attachment to external possessions (bāhya parigraha)."

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Internal (ābhyantara)** | Anger, pride, deceit, greed, attachment, aversion |
| **External (bāhya)** | Land, houses, gold, cattle, servants, clothes |

> "자이나교는 물질적이거나 감정적 소유물에 대한 집착이 열정으로 이어지고, 이것이 차례로 폭력으로 이어진다고 봅니다."
| 유형 | 설명 |
|-----|-----|
| **내적 (아뱐타라)** | 분노, 자만, 속임, 탐욕, 집착, 혐오 |
| **외적 (바흐야)** | 땅, 집, 금, 가축, 하인, 옷 |

## For Monastics vs. Laypersons | 출가자 vs. 재가자

> "Ethically, the vow of aparigraha (non-attachment) requires monks to renounce all property, while encouraging laypersons to limit their possessions and voluntarily limit their desires (iccha-parimana)."

> "Aparigraha, 'non-possessiveness': This includes non-attachment to material and psychological possessions, avoiding craving and greed. Jain monks and nuns completely renounce property and social relations, own nothing and are attached to no one."

### Lay Practice:
> "While the ascetic vow demands the total renunciation of property, the lay vow (Anuvrata) interprets non-possession as the 'voluntary limitation of desires' (Iccha-parimana)."

> "윤리적으로, 아파리그라하(무집착)의 서원은 수행자에게 모든 재산을 포기하도록 요구하고, 재가자에게는 소유물을 제한하고 자발적으로 욕망을 제한하도록(익챠-파리마나) 권장합니다."
### 재가자 실천:
> "금욕적 서원이 재산의 완전한 포기를 요구하는 반면, 재가 서원(아누브라타)은 무소유를 '욕망의 자발적 제한'으로 해석합니다."

## Liberation and the Soul | 해탈과 영혼

> "That aparigraha is a means to liberate the soul from the cycle of birth and death was first asserted by the first tirthankara in Jainism, Rishabhdeva."

### The Spiritual Goal:
Accumulating possessions:
- Creates karma
- Binds the soul
- Prevents moksha (liberation)

Practicing aparigraha:
- Reduces karma
- Frees the soul
- Leads to liberation

> "아파리그라하가 생사의 순환에서 영혼을 해방하는 수단이라는 것은 자이나교의 첫 번째 티르탕카라인 리샤브데바에 의해 처음 주장되었습니다."

## Charity and Social Ethics | 자선과 사회 윤리

> "The doctrine links non-possession directly to charity (dāna). Wealth exceeding the limits set by one's Iccha-parimana vow is viewed not as personal property, but as a resource held in trust for the welfare of the wider society."

> "This structural requirement led to the proliferation of Jain-funded libraries (bhandaras), animal hospitals (panjrapoles), and educational institutions."

### Social Impact:
| Institution | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| **Bhandaras** | Manuscript libraries |
| **Panjrapoles** | Animal shelters |
| **Educational institutions** | Learning centers |

> "이 교리는 무소유를 자선(다나)에 직접 연결합니다. 익챠-파리마나 서원에 의해 설정된 한계를 초과하는 부는 개인 재산이 아닌 더 넓은 사회의 복지를 위해 위탁된 자원으로 간주됩니다."
