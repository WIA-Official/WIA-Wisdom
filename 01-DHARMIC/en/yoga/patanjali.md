# Patanjali and the Yoga Sutras | 파탄잘리와 요가 수트라

> The Eight Limbs of Yoga: Stilling the Mind and Liberation of the Soul

---

## Overview | 개요

Patanjali was an ancient Indian sage traditionally credited as the compiler of the *Yoga Sutras*, the foundational text of Raja Yoga (the "royal yoga" of meditation). The *Yoga Sutras*, composed around 400 CE, systematize the philosophy and practice of yoga into 196 aphorisms organized into four chapters (*padas*).

> "Yoga is the stilling of the changing states of the mind" (*yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ*). This second sutra defines yoga as the cessation of mental fluctuations, allowing the seer to rest in its true nature.

Though little is known about Patanjali's life, his work has had an immeasurable influence on yoga practice and Hindu philosophy worldwide.

> "요가는 마음의 변화하는 상태를 멈추는 것이다" (*yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ*). 이 두 번째 수트라는 요가를 정신적 변동의 중지로 정의하여 보는 자가 그 진정한 본성 안에서 쉬도록 합니다.

## The Eight Limbs (Ashtanga) | 여덟 지분 (아쉬탕가)
> Patanjali's yoga is often called *Ashtanga Yoga* (eight-limbed yoga), comprising eight interconnected practices leading to liberation.

| Limb | Sanskrit | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Yama** | Ethical restraints |
| 2 | **Niyama** | Personal observances |
| 3 | **Āsana** | Posture |
| 4 | **Prāṇāyāma** | Breath control |
| 5 | **Pratyāhāra** | Sense withdrawal |
| 6 | **Dhāraṇā** | Concentration |
| 7 | **Dhyāna** | Meditation |
| 8 | **Samādhi** | Absorption |

> The eight limbs progress from external discipline to internal transformation, culminating in *samādhi*—complete absorption where the distinction between meditator, meditation, and object dissolves.

> 파탄잘리의 요가는 종종 해탈로 이끄는 여덟 가지 상호 연결된 수행을 포함하는 *아쉬탕가 요가* (여덟 지분 요가)라고 불립니다.

## The Five Yamas | 다섯 야마
> The yamas are ethical restraints that govern one's relationship with others and the world.

1. **Ahimsa** (अहिंसा): Non-violence
2. **Satya** (सत्य): Truthfulness
3. **Asteya** (अस्तेय): Non-stealing
4. **Brahmacharya** (ब्रह्मचर्य): Moderation/celibacy
5. **Aparigraha** (अपरिग्रह): Non-possessiveness

> These five constitute the "great vow" (*mahāvrata*), universal regardless of class, place, time, or circumstance.

> 야마는 타인과 세계와의 관계를 지배하는 윤리적 금지입니다.
---
## Chitta Vritti Nirodha | 마음 작용의 멈춤
> The goal of yoga is *chitta vritti nirodha*—the cessation of mental modifications. When the fluctuations of the mind cease, the seer (*purusha*) rests in its own nature.

> "Then the seer abides in its own form" (*tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe 'vasthānam*).

The five types of mental fluctuations (*vrittis*):
1. **Pramana**: Valid cognition
2. **Viparyaya**: Misconception
3. **Vikalpa**: Imagination
4. **Nidra**: Sleep
5. **Smriti**: Memory

> 요가의 목표는 *치타 브리티 니로다*—정신적 변용의 중지입니다. 마음의 변동이 멈출 때, 보는 자 (*푸루샤*)는 자신의 본성 안에서 쉽니다.
> "그러면 보는 자는 자신의 형태에 머문다" (*tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe 'vasthānam*).
---
## Samadhi: The Goal | 사마디: 목표
> Samadhi is the culmination of yogic practice—a state of complete absorption where subject and object merge.

Types of samadhi:
- **Samprajñāta** (with seed): Absorption with cognitive content
- **Asamprajñāta** (without seed): Absorption without content
- **Nirbīja**: Seedless samadhi, ultimate liberation

> "When the mind becomes like a pure crystal, it takes on the form of whatever object is placed before it—this is samadhi."

> 사마디는 요가 수행의 정점—주체와 객체가 합쳐지는 완전한 흡수 상태입니다.
---
## The Four Chapters | 네 장
1. **Samadhi Pada**: On absorption (51 sutras)
2. **Sadhana Pada**: On practice (55 sutras)
3. **Vibhuti Pada**: On supernatural powers (56 sutras)
4. **Kaivalya Pada**: On liberation (34 sutras)

> The sutras progress from defining yoga and its goal, through methods of practice, to the powers that arise, and finally to complete liberation (*kaivalya*).
