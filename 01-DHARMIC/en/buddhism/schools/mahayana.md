# Mahāyāna Buddhism | 대승불교 (大乘佛教)

> The Great Vehicle: The Path of Universal Liberation

---

## Overview | 개요

Mahāyāna (Sanskrit: "Great Vehicle") is one of the two main branches of Buddhism, distinguished by its emphasis on the bodhisattva path, universal salvation, and the teaching of emptiness (śūnyatā). While Theravāda focuses on individual liberation through the arhat ideal, Mahāyāna teaches that the highest goal is to become a bodhisattva—one who seeks enlightenment not for oneself alone but for the benefit of all sentient beings.

Emerging around the 1st century BCE, Mahāyāna became the dominant form of Buddhism in East Asia (China, Korea, Japan, Vietnam) and Tibet. It introduced new scriptures, expanded the Buddhist pantheon, and developed sophisticated philosophical systems that continue to influence Buddhist thought today.

---
## The Bodhisattva Ideal | 보살의 이상
The bodhisattva (菩薩, "awakening being") is central to Mahāyāna:

> "Instead of seeking complete enlightenment and nirvana, a bodhisattva vows to stay in the world to assist all beings."

### The Bodhisattva Vow
The Four Great Vows express this commitment:
1. Sentient beings are numberless; I vow to save them all
2. Delusions are endless; I vow to cut through them all
3. Dharma gates are boundless; I vow to enter them all
4. The Buddha Way is unsurpassable; I vow to attain it

## Emptiness (Śūnyatā) | 공 (空)
The doctrine of emptiness (śūnyatā) is Mahāyāna's most profound philosophical contribution:

> "By saying phenomena are 'empty' of solid, separate, and permanent existence, the teaching of emptiness points toward their true nature, which is impermanent, always changing, and dependent on causes and conditions."

### Key Points:
- All phenomena lack inherent, independent existence (svabhāva)
- Things exist only in dependence upon other things
- Emptiness is not nothingness but the absence of fixed essence
- Realizing emptiness leads to liberation from attachment

The Prajñāpāramitā (Perfection of Wisdom) sūtras, including the Heart Sūtra and Diamond Sūtra, expound this teaching.

> "현상이 견고하고, 분리되고, 영구한 존재가 '공'하다고 말함으로써, 공의 가르침은 무상하고, 항상 변하며, 인연에 의존하는 참된 본성을 가리킵니다."

## Skillful Means (Upāya) | 방편 (方便)
Upāya (skillful means) is the doctrine that enlightened beings adapt their teaching methods to the needs of different students:

> "Out of compassion, the Buddha adapts his teaching to whomever he is teaching."

The Lotus Sūtra famously teaches that all Buddhist paths are "skillful means" leading ultimately to the one Buddha vehicle. The bodhisattva uses any effective method—parables, gradual teachings, even apparent contradictions—to guide beings to awakening.

> "자비에서, 부처님은 가르치는 대상에 맞게 가르침을 조정합니다."
---
## Universal Salvation | 보편적 구원
Mahāyāna emphasizes that enlightenment is possible for all beings:

> "For Mahayana Buddhists, individual liberation is not enough, for none of us can truly be free until we all are."

This differs from early Buddhism's emphasis on individual liberation:
- All beings possess Buddha-nature (tathāgatagarbha)
- Liberation is interconnected—no one is truly free alone
- The bodhisattva works for the liberation of all

> "대승 불교도들에게 개인의 해탈만으로는 충분하지 않습니다. 우리 모두가 자유로워지기 전까지 아무도 진정으로 자유로울 수 없기 때문입니다."
