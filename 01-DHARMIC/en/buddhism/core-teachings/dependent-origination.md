# Dependent Origination (Pratītyasamutpāda) | 연기 (緣起)

> If This Exists, That Exists: The Web of Causation

---

## Overview | 개요

Pratītyasamutpāda (Sanskrit: प्रतीत्यसमुत्पाद), commonly translated as dependent origination or dependent arising, is a key doctrine in Buddhism shared by all schools. It states that all dharmas (phenomena) arise in dependence upon other dharmas: "if this exists, that exists; if this ceases to exist, that also ceases to exist."

This principle holds that all things arise in dependence upon multiple causes and conditions—nothing exists as a singular, independent entity. It is considered by many scholars to be the central insight of Buddhist philosophy, underlying teachings on suffering, no-self, and liberation.

---
## The Twelve Links (Nidānas) | 십이연기 (十二緣起)

The twelve links describe the chain of causality that keeps beings trapped in samsara:

| Link | Sanskrit | Korean | Description |
|------|----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Avidyā** | 무명 (無明) | Ignorance |
| 2 | **Saṃskāra** | 행 (行) | Mental formations |
| 3 | **Vijñāna** | 식 (識) | Consciousness |
| 4 | **Nāmarūpa** | 명색 (名色) | Name and form |
| 5 | **Ṣaḍāyatana** | 육입 (六入) | Six sense bases |
| 6 | **Sparśa** | 촉 (觸) | Contact |
| 7 | **Vedanā** | 수 (受) | Feeling |
| 8 | **Tṛṣṇā** | 애 (愛) | Craving |
| 9 | **Upādāna** | 취 (取) | Clinging |
| 10 | **Bhava** | 유 (有) | Becoming |
| 11 | **Jāti** | 생 (生) | Birth |
| 12 | **Jarāmaraṇa** | 노사 (老死) | Aging and death |

### How the Chain Works:

> "Ignorance is the root of suffering. It refers to the lack of understanding of the true nature of reality, preventing us from seeing the impermanence of life and the interconnectedness of all things."

> "무명은 고통의 뿌리입니다. 그것은 현실의 진정한 본성에 대한 이해 부족을 말하며, 삶의 무상함과 모든 것의 상호연결성을 보지 못하게 합니다."
---
## Classification | 분류

The twelve links can be classified into four categories:

| Category | Links |
|----------|-------|
| **Causal branches that project** | Ignorance, formation, causal consciousness |
| **Projected branches** | Resultant consciousness, name-form, sense bases, contact, feeling |
| **Causal branches that actualize** | Craving, grasping, becoming |
| **Actualized branches** | Birth, aging-and-death |

---
## Philosophical Significance | 철학적 의의

### Ontological Implications:
> "According to Bhikkhu Bodhi, Peter Harvey and Paul Williams, dependent arising can be understood as an ontological principle; that is, a theory to explain the nature and relations of being, becoming, existence and ultimate reality."

### No-Self (Anātman):
> "The lack of an intrinsic, unchanging self is emphasized by Pratityasamutpada. The notion of causation in this context indicates that the self is a construction that results from the interaction of multiple factors."

### No Creator God:
> "The important corollary of this teaching is that there is nothing that comes into being through its own power or volition, and there are therefore no entities or metaphysical realities such as God or a soul that transcend the causal nexus."

### 존재론적 함의:
> "빅쿠 보디, 피터 하비, 폴 윌리엄스에 따르면, 연기는 존재론적 원리로 이해될 수 있습니다."
### 무아 (無我):
> "내재적이고 변하지 않는 자아의 부재가 연기에 의해 강조됩니다. 이 맥락에서 인과 개념은 자아가 다양한 요소의 상호작용에서 비롯된 구성물임을 나타냅니다."
---
## Liberation | 해탈

> "Since this complex chain of causation is always said to give rise to suffering, the deactivation of any of the twelve links is bound to break the causal process and to eliminate suffering."

> "Ultimately, the goal of Buddhist practice is to reach nirvana—a state of liberation where the chain of dependent origination no longer operates."

> "이 복잡한 인과의 사슬이 항상 고통을 일으킨다고 하므로, 십이연기 중 어느 하나라도 비활성화하면 인과 과정을 끊고 고통을 제거할 수 있습니다."
> "궁극적으로, 불교 수행의 목표는 연기의 사슬이 더 이상 작동하지 않는 해탈의 상태인 열반에 도달하는 것입니다."
---
## Mahayana and Theravada | 대승과 상좌부

> "In the Mahayana tradition, pratityasamutpada is used to refer to the general principle of interdependent causation, whereas in the Theravada tradition, paticcasamuppāda is used to refer to the twelve nidānas."

### Mahayana Extension:
> "Pratityasamutpada's scope was expanded in Mahayana texts to encompass not only individual suffering but also the interconnectedness of all phenomena. The idea of 'emptiness' (shunyata) was first presented in Mahayana texts, highlighting the absence of inherent existence in all things."

> "대승 전통에서 연기는 상호의존적 인과의 일반 원리를 지칭하는 데 사용되고, 상좌부 전통에서는 십이연기를 지칭하는 데 사용됩니다."
### 대승의 확장:
> "연기의 범위는 대승 문헌에서 개인의 고통뿐만 아니라 모든 현상의 상호연결성을 포괄하도록 확장되었습니다."
