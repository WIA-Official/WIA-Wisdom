# Three Marks of Existence | 삼법인 (三法印)

> Impermanence, Suffering, Non-Self

---

## Overview | 개요

In Buddhism, the three marks of existence (Pali: tilakkhaṇa; Sanskrit: trilakṣaṇa) are three characteristics shared by all existence and beings: **anicca** (impermanence), **dukkha** (suffering/unsatisfactoriness), and **anattā** (non-self). Understanding these marks is essential for liberation from suffering.

Piyadassi Thera writes that impermanence is "the very core of the Buddha's teaching, being also the basis for the other two characteristics." These three truths describe the fundamental nature of conditioned existence.

---
## Anicca (Impermanence) | 무상 (無常)

> "Impermanence (anicca) refers to the fact that all conditioned things are impermanent, constantly changing, in a constant state of flux."

### Key Points:
- All phenomena arise and pass away
- Nothing remains static
- Change is the only constant

### Examples:
| Level | Impermanence |
|-------|--------------|
| **Physical** | Bodies age, objects decay |
| **Mental** | Thoughts arise and cease |
| **Relational** | Relationships change |
| **Cosmic** | Stars, worlds come and go |

> "무상(아닛짜)은 모든 조건지어진 것들이 무상하고, 끊임없이 변화하며, 항상 유동 상태에 있다는 사실을 말합니다."

## Dukkha (Suffering) | 고 (苦)

> "Dukkha is often translated as suffering, but it also encompasses frustration, dissatisfaction, and discomfort. It arises due to clinging to impermanent things and misunderstanding reality."

### Three Types:
| Type | Pali | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Direct suffering** | Dukkha-dukkha | Pain, illness, loss |
| **Suffering of change** | Viparinama-dukkha | Losing what we love |
| **Existential suffering** | Sankhara-dukkha | Conditioned nature of reality |

> "둑카는 흔히 고통으로 번역되지만, 좌절, 불만족, 불편함도 포함합니다. 무상한 것에 집착하고 현실을 오해하기 때문에 생깁니다."
### 세 가지 유형:
| 유형 | 팔리어 | 설명 |
|-----|-------|-----|
| **직접적 고통** | 둑카-둑카 | 아픔, 질병, 상실 |
| **변화의 고통** | 비파리나마-둑카 | 사랑하는 것을 잃음 |
| **존재론적 고통** | 상카라-둑카 | 현실의 조건지어진 본성 |

## Anattā (Non-Self) | 무아 (無我)

> "The anattā doctrine of Buddhism denies that there is anything permanent in any person to call one's Self, and that a belief in a Self is a source of dukkha."

> "Unlike many traditions that speak of a soul or eternal essence, Buddhism teaches that what we call 'self' is merely a bundle of conditions—body, feelings, perceptions, thoughts, and consciousness—constantly shifting."

### The Five Aggregates (Skandhas):
| Aggregate | Pali | Content |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Form** | Rūpa | Physical body |
| **Feeling** | Vedanā | Pleasant/unpleasant/neutral |
| **Perception** | Saññā | Recognition, identification |
| **Mental formations** | Saṅkhāra | Volitions, impulses |
| **Consciousness** | Viññāṇa | Awareness |

> "불교의 무아 교리는 어떤 사람에게든 자아라고 부를 영구적인 것이 없으며, 자아에 대한 믿음이 둑카의 원인임을 부정합니다."

|---|-------|-----|

| **행** | 상카라 | 의지, 충동 |

## Interrelationship | 상호관계

> "Because everything is impermanent (Anicca), there is no fixed self (Anattā). Because we cling to the impermanent, we experience suffering (Dukkha). When we see through the illusion of permanence and self, suffering begins to dissolve."

### The Logic:
1. All is impermanent → Nothing has lasting essence
2. We cling to what changes → We suffer
3. Understanding this → Liberation

> "The concept of humans being subject to delusion about the three marks, this delusion resulting in suffering, and removal of that delusion resulting in the end of dukkha, is a central theme in the Buddhist Four Noble Truths."

> "모든 것이 무상하기(아닛짜) 때문에 고정된 자아가 없습니다(아낫따). 무상한 것에 집착하기 때문에 고통을 경험합니다(둑카). 영원함과 자아의 환상을 꿰뚫어 볼 때 고통이 녹아내리기 시작합니다."

## Practice | 수행

> "The Buddha's advice to monks is that they should try to maintain the awareness of Anicca, Dukkha or Anatta in all postures, whether sitting, standing, walking or lying down."

### Benefits of Insight:
> "The three characteristics are of great importance because direct experiential insight into one of these characteristics opens the gate to Nibbāna."

> "By understanding that everything is impermanent, unsatisfactory and without an identifiable self, the mind can be released and become completely free from desire, hatred and ignorance."

> "붓다의 수행자에 대한 조언은 앉거나, 서거나, 걷거나, 누워 있을 때 모든 자세에서 무상, 고, 또는 무아에 대한 자각을 유지하려고 노력해야 한다는 것입니다."
### 통찰의 이점:
> "삼법인은 이 특성 중 하나에 대한 직접적 경험적 통찰이 열반의 문을 열기 때문에 매우 중요합니다."
