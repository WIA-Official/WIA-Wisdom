# Naam Japna | 나암 자프나 (ਨਾਮ ਜਪਣਾ)

> Meditation on the Divine Name: The First Pillar of Sikhism

---

## Overview | 개요

Naam Japna (Gurmukhi: ਨਾਮ ਜਪਣਾ, "meditating on God's name") is the first of the Three Pillars of Sikhism established by Guru Nanak Dev Ji (1469-1539). It involves the constant remembrance, recitation, and meditation on the divine name—particularly "Waheguru"—as the path to spiritual awakening and union with God.

Naam Japna is not mere repetition of words, but a deep practice of consciousness transformation through which the devotee's mind becomes immersed in divine awareness, leading to liberation from the cycle of rebirth.

## Waheguru: The Divine Name | 와헤구루: 신성한 이름

### Meaning:
> "The mantra 'Waheguru' derives from Guru Nanak's teachings as an exclamation of wonder ('Wah' meaning 'wow' or 'astonishment' at the Divine), emerging from his visionary experience of the Creator's infinite glory."

### Components:
- **Wah** (ਵਾਹ): "Wonderful" or "Wondrous"
- **Guru** (ਗੁਰੂ): "Teacher" or "Enlightener" (from darkness to light)

Together: "The Wonderful Lord" or "Praise to the Divine Teacher"

### Practice:
> "The practice involves meditation, vocal rendition of Shabads or hymns from the Sri Guru Granth Sahib, or the recitation of the word 'Waheguru,' signifying the Wonderful Lord."

### 의미:
> "와헤구루 만트라는 구루 나낙의 가르침에서 유래하며 신성에 대한 경이("와"는 '와' 또는 경탄을 의미)의 외침으로, 창조주의 무한한 영광에 대한 비전적 경험에서 나왔습니다."

## The Practice of Naam Japna | 나암 자프나의 수행

### What It Involves:
> "Naam Japna involves meditation on God through reciting, chanting, singing and constant remembrance followed by deep study and comprehension of God's Name and virtues."

### Inner Transformation:
> "The inner voice of the Sikh thus stays immersed in praises and appreciation of the Creator and the ONE ETERNAL GOD Waheguru and his WILL."

### Methods:
| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Simran** | Silent meditation on Waheguru |
| **Kirtan** | Devotional singing of hymns |
| **Path** | Reading from Guru Granth Sahib |
| **Ardas** | Formal prayer |

### 포함하는 것:

## Guru Nanak's Enlightenment | 구루 나낙의 깨달음

### The River Experience:
> "Guru Nanak Dev Ji experienced a profound enlightenment at the age of 30 in 1499 CE, when he disappeared into the waters of the Kali Bein River near Sultanpur Lodhi for three days, emerging with a divine mission to propagate the remembrance of God, known as Naam Japo."

### His Message:
> "Nanak is said to have travelled far and wide across Asia teaching people the message of Ik Onkar (ੴ, 'One God'), who dwells in every one of his creations and constitutes the eternal Truth."

### 강에서의 경험:
> "구루 나낙 데브 지는 1499년 30세에 술탄푸르 로디 근처 칼리 베인 강물 속으로 사라져 사흘 후 나암 자포로 알려진 하나님에 대한 기억을 전파하는 신성한 사명을 갖고 나타나며 심오한 깨달음을 경험했습니다."

## Salvation Through Naam | 나암을 통한 구원

> "Guru Nanak's message can be briefly summarized as a doctrine of salvation through disciplined meditation on the divine name. Salvation is understood in terms of escape from the transmigratory round of death and rebirth to a mystical union with God."

### Inward Focus:
> "Meditation must be strictly inward, and all external aids such as idols, temples, mosques, scriptures, and set prayers are explicitly rejected."

### Formless Devotion:
> "Central to Guru Nanak's innovations was his rejection of idol worship and ritualistic practices, instead advocating Naam Japo as a formless devotion focused on meditating upon God's inherent attributes like truth, compassion, and oneness."

> "구루 나낙의 메시지는 신성한 이름에 대한 규율된 명상을 통한 구원의 교리로 간략히 요약될 수 있습니다. 구원은 죽음과 재생의 윤회에서 탈출하여 하나님과 신비로운 합일에 이르는 것으로 이해됩니다."
