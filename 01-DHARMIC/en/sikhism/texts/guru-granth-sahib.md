# Guru Granth Sahib | 구루 그란트 사히브

> The Eternal Living Guru

---

## Overview | 개요

The Guru Granth Sahib is the central holy religious scripture of Sikhism, regarded by Sikhs as the final, sovereign and eternal Guru following the lineage of the ten human gurus of the religion. The Sikhs do not regard this as their "holy book" but as their perpetual and current "guru", guide or master.

It was called Adi Granth until Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth and final guru in human form, conferred on it the title of the guru in 1708, after which it was called Sri Guru Granth Sahib.

---
## Compilation History | 편찬 역사

### The First Version:
> "The Adi Granth, its first rendition, was compiled by the fifth guru, Guru Arjan (1564–1606). Its compilation was completed on 29 August 1604 and first installed inside the Golden Temple in Amritsar on 1 September 1604."

### Contents:
> "Guru Arjan included his own hymns and those of his predecessors, the Gurus Nanak, Angad, Amar Das, and Ram Das, and a selection of devotional songs of both Hindu and Islamic saints (notably the poet Kabīr)."

### Final Addition:
> "In 1704 CE the 10th and last Guru, Gobind Singh, added the hymns of his predecessor, Guru Tegh Bahadur, and enjoined that after his own death the Granth would take the place of the Guru."

### 첫 번째 버전:
> "첫 번째 판본인 아디 그란트는 다섯 번째 구루인 구루 아르잔(1564-1606)에 의해 편찬되었습니다. 편찬은 1604년 8월 29일에 완료되었고 1604년 9월 1일 암리차르의 황금 사원 안에 처음 설치되었습니다."
### 최종 추가:
> "1704년 열 번째이자 마지막 구루인 고빈드 싱이 그의 전임자인 구루 테그 바하두르의 찬가를 추가하고, 자신의 죽음 후에 그란트가 구루의 자리를 대신할 것이라고 명령했습니다."

## Structure and Content | 구조와 내용

> "The Granth has 1430 Ang Sahib (ang meaning limb since the Guru Granth Sahib is not a book but it is the eternal Guru for Sikhs) divided into 39 chapters. All copies are exactly alike. The Sikhs are forbidden from making any changes to the text."

### Organization:
- Opens with **Mul Mantra** (Basic Prayer)
- Followed by **Japji** (the most important Sikh scripture)
- Hymns arranged by **ragas** (musical modes)

### Statistics:
- 1,430 pages (Ang)
- Nearly 6,000 hymns
- 39 chapters
- Multiple authors

> "It is a collection of nearly 6,000 hymns of the Sikh Gurus (religious leaders) and various early and medieval saints of different religions and castes."

> "그란트는 39장으로 나뉜 1430 앙 사히브가 있습니다(앙은 사지를 의미하며, 구루 그란트 사히브가 책이 아니라 시크교도들에게 영원한 구루이기 때문입니다). 모든 사본은 정확히 동일합니다."

## Religious Significance | 종교적 의의

> "The Adi Granth is the central object of worship in all gurdwaras (Sikh temples) and is accorded the reverence paid a living Guru. It is ritually opened in the morning and wrapped up and put away for the night."

### Unique Features:
> "This Granth is the only world religious scripture which was compiled during the life time of its compiler. All other world scriptures were compiled many years after the death of the prophet."

### Continuous Reading:
> "On special occasions continuous readings of it are held, which last from 2 to 15 days."

> "아디 그란트는 모든 구르드와라(시크교 사원)에서 예배의 중심 대상이며 살아있는 구루에게 바치는 경의를 받습니다. 아침에 의식적으로 열리고 밤에 감싸서 치워집니다."
### 독특한 특징:
> "이 그란트는 편찬자의 생전에 편찬된 유일한 세계 종교 경전입니다. 다른 모든 세계 경전은 예언자의 죽음 후 많은 해가 지난 후에 편찬되었습니다."

## The Mul Mantra | 물 만트라

The opening declaration of the Guru Granth Sahib:

**Gurmukhi:** ੴ ਸਤਿ ਨਾਮੁ ਕਰਤਾ ਪੁਰਖੁ ਨਿਰਭਉ ਨਿਰਵੈਰੁ ਅਕਾਲ ਮੂਰਤਿ ਅਜੂਨੀ ਸੈਭੰ ਗੁਰ ਪ੍ਰਸਾਦਿ ॥

**Translation:** "One God, Truth by Name, Creative Principle, Without Fear, Without Enmity, Timeless Form, Unborn, Self-existent, By the Guru's Grace."

## Original Manuscript | 원본 사본

> "The original copy of the scripture, called Adi Granth, compiled and authenticated by Guru Arjan Dev still exists today and is kept at Kartarpur which is a town about 15 km north west of the city of Jalandhar, Punjab, India."

> "구루 아르잔 데브가 편찬하고 인증한 아디 그란트라고 불리는 경전의 원본 사본은 오늘날에도 존재하며 인도 펀잡 잘란다르 시에서 북서쪽으로 약 15km 떨어진 카르타르푸르 마을에 보관되어 있습니다."
