# Pure Land Buddhism | 정토종 (淨土宗)

> Rebirth in the Buddha's Pure Land Through Faith and Practice
> 믿음과 수행을 통한 부처의 정토에 왕생

---

## Overview | 개요

**English:**
Pure Land Buddhism is one of the most widely practiced traditions of Buddhism in East Asia, focused on achieving rebirth in a Pure Land—a realm created by a Buddha's merit where conditions are ideal for attaining enlightenment. The tradition centers on Amitābha Buddha (Sanskrit: "Infinite Light") and his Western Pure Land called Sukhāvatī ("Land of Bliss").

The central practice involves reciting Amitābha's name (nianfo in Chinese, nembutsu in Japanese), with the faith that sincere devotion enables rebirth in his Pure Land, where ultimate enlightenment becomes achievable for all beings. This tradition made Buddhism accessible to laypeople, emphasizing "other-power" (tariki) over self-effort.

**한국어:**
정토종은 동아시아에서 가장 널리 수행되는 불교 전통 중 하나로, 정토—부처의 공덕으로 창조된 깨달음을 얻기 이상적인 조건의 영역—에 왕생하는 것을 목표로 합니다. 이 전통은 아미타불(산스크리트어: "무한한 빛")과 극락정토 수카바티("안락의 땅")를 중심으로 합니다.

핵심 수행은 아미타불의 이름을 외우는 것(중국어: 염불, 일본어: 넨부츠)으로, 진심 어린 헌신이 정토에 왕생할 수 있게 한다는 믿음에 기반합니다. 이 전통은 자력보다 "타력"을 강조하여 재가자들이 불교에 접근할 수 있게 했습니다.

## The Pure Land | 정토

**English:**

> "The Pure Land is a realm where all conditions are ideal for attaining enlightenment."

### Sukhāvatī (Land of Bliss):
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Creator** | Amitābha Buddha through countless eons of merit |
| **Location** | Western direction from our world |
| **Conditions** | No suffering, perfect environment for practice |
| **Access** | Through faith and nembutsu practice |

### Characteristics:
- Ground of gold and precious jewels
- Lotus flowers of immeasurable size
- Celestial music and fragrance
- Direct teaching from Amitābha Buddha

**한국어:**

| 특징 | 설명 |
|-----|------|
| **창조주** | 무수겁의 공덕을 통한 아미타불 |
| **위치** | 우리 세계에서 서방 |
| **조건** | 고통 없음, 수행에 완벽한 환경 |
| **접근** | 믿음과 염불 수행을 통해 |

## Core Practice: Nembutsu | 핵심 수행: 염불

**English:**

> "Namu Amida Butsu" (Japanese) / "Namo Amituofo" (Chinese)
> "I take refuge in Amitābha Buddha"

### Forms of Practice:
1. **Vocal Recitation** - Chanting the Buddha's name
2. **Silent Recitation** - Mental repetition
3. **Visualization** - Contemplating the Pure Land
4. **Prostrations** - Physical devotion

> "The recitation of Amitābha Buddha's name enables one to grow closer to Waheguru. By reciting with sincerity and single-minded devotion, rebirth in the Pure Land becomes possible."

**한국어:**

### 수행 형태:
1. **칭명염불** - 부처님 이름 소리내어 외움
2. **묵념** - 마음으로 반복
3. **관상** - 정토를 관상함
4. **절** - 신체적 헌신

## Historical Development | 역사적 발전

**English:**

### Key Figures:
| Figure | Era | Contribution |
|--------|-----|--------------|
| **Huiyuan** | 334–416 | Founded first Pure Land community on Mount Lu |
| **Shandao** | 613–681 | Systematized Pure Land practice in China |
| **Hōnen** | 1133–1212 | Established Jōdo-shū in Japan |
| **Shinran** | 1173–1263 | Founded Jōdo Shinshū (True Pure Land) |
| **Genshin** | 942–1017 | Wrote *Ōjōyōshū* on Pure Land teaching |

### Three Pure Land Sutras:
1. Larger Sukhāvatīvyūha Sūtra (무량수경)
2. Shorter Amitābha Sūtra (아미타경)
3. Amitāyurdhyāna Sūtra (관무량수경)

|-----|-----|------|

## Theology | 신학

**English:**

### Self-Power vs. Other-Power:
| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Jiriki (自力)** | Self-power: Relying on one's own effort |
| **Tariki (他力)** | Other-power: Relying on Amitābha's compassion |

> "Hōnen believed that most people were incapable of obtaining buddhahood through their own efforts but were dependent on Amida's help."

### The Primal Vow:
Amitābha's 18th vow promises that all beings who sincerely desire rebirth in his Pure Land and call upon his name will be reborn there.

**한국어:**

| 개념 | 설명 |
|-----|------|
| **자력** | 자신의 노력에 의지함 |
| **타력** | 아미타불의 자비에 의지함 |
