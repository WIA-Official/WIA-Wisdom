## Overview | 개요

Shamanism is a system of religious practice that is historically often associated with Indigenous and tribal societies. It involves belief that shamans, with a connection to the otherworld, have the power to heal the sick, communicate with spirits, and escort souls of the dead to the afterlife.

Shamanism is believed to have originated in the ancient cultures of Siberia and Central Asia, particularly among the indigenous tribes of Siberia and Mongolia. The term "shaman" itself derives from the Tungus word *saman*, meaning "one who knows."

At its core, shamanism's foundational belief system is animism—the understanding that every stone, tree, and creature carries a spirit. This worldview sees the cosmos as alive, ensouled, and relational, where humans are one type of person among many in a world filled with other-than-human persons.

## Animism: The Living World | 애니미즘: 살아있는 세계
> Animism is a worldview or belief system that holds that all things—animals, plants, rocks, rivers, weather, places—possess spirit, consciousness, or soul. Animists perceive the world as alive, ensouled, and relational, where humans are one type of person among many.

> Animism is not a practice but a way of perceiving and relating to the world.

Key principles of the animistic worldview:
- **Universal Spirit**: Everything possesses consciousness or spirit
- **Relational Ontology**: Beings exist in webs of relationship
- **Reciprocity**: Humans must maintain proper relationships with spirits
- **Permeable Boundaries**: The spirit world and material world interpenetrate

> Animism is often the worldview within which shamanism operates, but one can be animist without being a shaman.

> 애니미즘은 모든 것—동물, 식물, 바위, 강, 날씨, 장소—이 영, 의식, 또는 영혼을 가지고 있다고 주장하는 세계관 또는 믿음 체계입니다. 애니미스트들은 세계를 살아있고, 영혼이 깃들어 있으며, 관계적인 것으로 인식하고, 인간은 많은 유형의 인격 중 하나입니다.
> 애니미즘은 수행이 아니라 세계를 인식하고 관계하는 방식입니다.

## The Shaman's Role | 샤먼의 역할
> Shamans are spiritual leaders who act as intermediaries between the human and spirit worlds, using their knowledge and abilities to communicate with spirits and harness their power. They play a central role in animistic societies as spiritual guides, healers, and advisors.

The shaman's functions include:
- **Healing**: Treating illness at its spiritual root
- **Divination**: Seeking knowledge from spirits
- **Psychopomp**: Guiding souls of the dead
- **Maintaining Balance**: Mediating human-spirit relations
- **Weather Working**: Influencing natural forces
- **Soul Retrieval**: Recovering lost soul parts

> 샤먼들은 인간과 영의 세계 사이에서 중개자 역할을 하는 영적 지도자로, 자신의 지식과 능력을 사용하여 영들과 소통하고 그들의 힘을 활용합니다. 그들은 애니미즘 사회에서 영적 안내자, 치유사, 조언자로서 중심 역할을 합니다.

## Spirit World and Cosmology | 영의 세계와 우주론
> Shamanism is based on the premise that the visible world is pervaded by invisible forces or spirits which affect the lives of the living. Although the causes of disease lie in the spiritual realm, inspired by malicious spirits, both spiritual and physical methods are used to heal.

Typical shamanic cosmology includes:

### Three Worlds | 세 세계
- **Upper World**: Realm of celestial spirits, ancestors, guides
- **Middle World**: The ordinary reality, physical world
- **Lower World**: Realm of power animals, nature spirits

### The World Tree | 세계수
The axis connecting all three realms, often depicted as a great tree whose roots reach the underworld and branches touch the heavens.

> 샤머니즘은 보이는 세계가 살아있는 자의 삶에 영향을 미치는 보이지 않는 힘이나 영들로 가득 차 있다는 전제에 기초합니다. 질병의 원인이 영적 영역에, 악의적 영들에 의해 고무되어 있지만, 영적이고 물리적인 방법 모두가 치유에 사용됩니다.

## Healing Practices | 치유 수행
> Shamans are said to treat ailments and illnesses by mending the soul. Alleviating traumas affecting the soul or spirit restores the physical body of the individual to balance and wholeness.

> Shamans direct and move energy to restore the harmony within the individual, between the individual and the community, and between the community and the spirit world.

> Commonly, a shaman "enters the body" of the patient to confront the spiritual infirmity and heals by banishing the infectious spirit.

Healing techniques include:
- **Extraction**: Removing intrusive spirits or energies
- **Soul Retrieval**: Recovering lost soul fragments
- **Power Animal Retrieval**: Restoring guardian spirits
- **Herbal Medicine**: Many shamans have expert knowledge of medicinal plants

> Many shamans learn directly from the plants, harnessing their effects and healing properties, after obtaining permission from the indwelling or patron spirits.

> 샤먼들은 영혼을 고침으로써 질병을 치료한다고 합니다. 영혼이나 정신에 영향을 미치는 트라우마를 완화하면 개인의 물리적 몸이 균형과 온전함으로 회복됩니다.
> 샤먼들은 개인 내의, 개인과 공동체 사이의, 공동체와 영의 세계 사이의 조화를 회복하기 위해 에너지를 지휘하고 움직입니다.

## Shamanic Techniques | 샤먼적 기술
> The drum is used by shamans of several peoples in Siberia. The beating of the drum allows the shaman to achieve an altered state of consciousness or to travel on a journey between the physical and spiritual worlds.

Core techniques:
- **Drumming**: Inducing trance through rhythmic percussion
- **Chanting**: Sacred songs to invoke spirits
- **Dance**: Ecstatic movement to shift consciousness
- **Fasting**: Purification and vision-seeking
- **Plant Medicines**: Entheogens for spiritual journeys
- **Ritual Objects**: Sacred tools like drums, rattles, costumes

> In animism, rituals are performed to maintain relationships between humans and spirits. Indigenous peoples often perform these rituals to appease the spirits and request their assistance during activities such as hunting and healing.

> 북은 시베리아 여러 민족의 샤먼들이 사용합니다. 북의 연주는 샤먼이 변성 의식 상태에 도달하거나 물리적 세계와 영적 세계 사이를 여행할 수 있게 합니다.

## Cultural Respect | 문화적 존중
> Despite structural implications of colonialism and imperialism that have limited the ability of Indigenous peoples to practice traditional spiritualities, many communities are undergoing resurgence through self-determination and the reclamation of dynamic traditions.

> It's important to approach all indigenous spiritual traditions with deep respect, support indigenous communities, and never commercialize or appropriate what doesn't belong to you. Whether adopting an animist worldview or studying shamanic traditions respectfully, these are living practices belonging to real people and cultures.

> 토착민들이 전통적 영성을 실천하는 능력을 제한한 식민주의와 제국주의의 구조적 함의에도 불구하고, 많은 공동체가 자기 결정과 역동적 전통의 회복을 통해 부흥을 겪고 있습니다.
> 모든 토착 영적 전통에 깊은 존경으로 접근하고, 토착 공동체를 지원하며, 자신에게 속하지 않은 것을 결코 상업화하거나 전유하지 않는 것이 중요합니다.
